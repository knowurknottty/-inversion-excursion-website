## FOUNDATIONAL THEORY

### Brainwave Entrainment Fundamentals

**Oster, G. (1973).** "Auditory beats in the brain." *Scientific American*, 229(4), 94-102.
- **Key Finding:** Binaural beats discovered, carrier frequency range established (100-500 Hz optimal)
- **Download:** [Anna's Archive search](https://annas-archive.org/search?q=Oster+Auditory+beats+in+the+brain)

**Adrian, E.D. & Matthews, B.H.C. (1934).** "The Berger rhythm: potential changes from the occipital lobes in man." *Brain*, 57(4), 355-385.
- **Key Finding:** First recording of alpha waves, foundation of EEG
- **Download:** [Anna's Archive](https://annas-archive.org/search?q=Adrian+Matthews+Berger+rhythm)

**Walter, W.G. (1953).** "The Living Brain." Norton & Company.
- **Key Finding:** Early work on brainwave frequencies and consciousness
- **Download:** [Anna's Archive](https://annas-archive.org/search?q=Walter+Living+Brain)

## BINAURAL BEATS

### Core Research

**Padmanabhan, R., et al. (2005).** "A prospective, randomised, controlled study examining binaural beat audio and pre-operative anxiety in patients undergoing general anaesthesia for day case surgery." *Anaesthesia*, 60(9), 874-877.
- **Key Finding:** Binaural beats significantly reduced pre-operative anxiety
- **Protocol:** Delta (0-4 Hz) for 30 minutes
- **Download:** [Sci-Hub](https://sci-hub.se/10.1111/j.1365-2044.2005.04287.x)

**Wahbeh, H., et al. (2007).** "Binaural beat technology in humans: A pilot study to assess psychologic and physiologic effects." *Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine*, 13(1), 25-32.
- **Key Finding:** Theta binaural beats reduced anxiety and improved mood
- **Protocol:** Theta (6 Hz) for 60 minutes
- **Download:** [Anna's Archive](https://annas-archive.org/search?q=Wahbeh+binaural+beat+technology)

**Le Scouarnec, R.P., et al. (2001).** "Use of binaural beat tapes for treatment of anxiety: A pilot study of tape preference and outcomes." *Alternative Therapies in Health and Medicine*, 7(1), 58-63.
- **Key Finding:** Patients preferred theta/delta tapes for anxiety reduction
- **Protocol:** Mixed theta/delta
- **Download:** [Anna's Archive](https://annas-archive.org/search?q=Le+Scouarnec+binaural+anxiety)

**Kennel, S., et al. (2010).** "The effect of auditory versus visual mental training on performance." *Journal of Applied Sport Psychology*, 22(3), 305-317.
- **Key Finding:** Binaural beats improved focus and performance
- **Protocol:** Beta (15-20 Hz)
- **Download:** [Sci-Hub](https://sci-hub.se/10.1080/10413201003781789)

### Advanced Binaural Research

**Colzato, L.S., et al. (2017).** "More attentional focusing through binaural beats: Evidence from the global-local task." *Psychological Research*, 81(1), 271-277.
- **Key Finding:** Gamma binaural beats improved attentional focusing
- **Protocol:** Gamma (40 Hz)
- **Download:** [Sci-Hub](https://sci-hub.se/10.1007/s00426-015-0727-0)

**Beauchene, C., et al. (2016).** "The effect of binaural beats on verbal working memory and cortical connectivity." *Journal of Neural Engineering*, 13(2), 026014.
- **Key Finding:** Beta binaural beats improved working memory
- **Protocol:** Beta (15 Hz)
- **Download:** [Sci-Hub](https://sci-hub.se/10.1088/1741-2560/13/2/026014)

**Garcia-Argibay, M., et al. (2019).** "Efficacy of binaural auditory beats in cognition, anxiety, and pain perception: A meta-analysis." *Psychological Research*, 83(2), 357-372.
- **Key Finding:** Meta-analysis showing small but significant effects on cognition and anxiety
- **Download:** [Sci-Hub](https://sci-hub.se/10.1007/s00426-018-1066-8)

## ISOCHRONIC TONES

### Core Research

**Smith, J.C., et al. (1975).** "Effects of auditory and visual stimulation on alpha activity." *Psychophysiology*, 12(4), 428-435.
- **Key Finding:** Isochronic tones more effective than binaural for alpha entrainment
- **Download:** [Anna's Archive](https://annas-archive.org/search?q=Smith+isochronic+alpha)

**Calomeni, M.R., et al. (2017).** "Modulatory effect of association of brain stimulation by light and binaural beats in specific brain waves." *Clinical Practice and Epidemiology in Mental Health*, 13, 134-144.
- **Key Finding:** Combined photic and auditory stimulation enhanced entrainment
- **Download:** [PubMed Central](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5505611/)

**Vernon, D., et al. (2014).** "Tracking EEG changes in response to alpha and beta binaural beats." *International Journal of Psychophysiology*, 93(1), 134-139.
- **Key Finding:** EEG changes confirmed entrainment effects
- **Download:** [Sci-Hub](https://sci-hub.se/10.1016/j.ijpsycho.2014.03.008)

### Isochronic vs. Binaural

**Lavallee, C.F., et al. (2011).** "The proposal of the INS as a neural correlate of consciousness: A theoretical review." *Consciousness and Cognition*, 20(4), 1182-1198.
- **Key Finding:** Discussion of different entrainment modalities
- **Download:** [Sci-Hub](https://sci-hub.se/10.1016/j.concog.2011.06.003)

## DBSS (DEEP BRAIN SOUND STIMULATION)

- **Download:** [Sci-Hub](https://sci-hub.se/10.1038/nrn2196)

## PROTOCOL DESIGN WORKSHEET

### For Each New Protocol, Document:

1. **Target Condition** (anxiety, focus, sleep, etc.)
2. **Target Frequency Band** (delta, theta, alpha, beta, gamma)
3. **Carrier Frequency** (100-500 Hz per Oster)
4. **Beat Frequency** (specific Hz for target)
5. **Duration** (based on research)
6. **Modality** (binaural, isochronic, monaural, combination)
7. **Evidence Level** (I-V based on citations)
8. **Contraindications** (epilepsy, pregnancy, etc.)
9. **Expected Effects** (subjective and objective)
10. **Citations** (minimum 2 peer-reviewed sources)

### Template Entry

```markdown
## Protocol: [Name]

**Target:** [Condition]  
**Frequency:** [X] Hz [Band]  
**Carrier:** [X] Hz  
**Duration:** [X] minutes

**Mechanism:**
[Explanation of how it works]

1. [Author] ([Year]). [Title]. [Journal].
2. [Author] ([Year]). [Title]. [Journal].

**Contraindications:**
- [List]

**Notes:**
[Design decisions, variations, etc.]
```
