# Patent Landscape Assessment: Brainwave Entrainment, 40Hz Gamma, Binaural Beats & Cognitive Enhancement

**Prepared for:** SynSync Pro  
**Date:** March 3, 2026  
**Scope:** Freedom to Operate (FTO) Analysis & IP Strategy

---

## Executive Summary

The brainwave entrainment and cognitive enhancement space has a **complex but navigable IP landscape**. While there are significant foundational patents that have expired (creating public domain opportunities), there are also **active blocking patents** in the 40Hz gamma stimulation space, particularly around medical/therapeutic applications. The good news: **pure binaural beats technology is largely in the public domain**, and there are clear design-around strategies available.

**Key Finding:** SynSync has viable paths forward, but must carefully navigate around Cognito Therapeutics' patent portfolio in the medical device space and avoid infringing on active Hemi-Sync trademarks and potentially extended patents.

---

## 1. Blocking Patents Analysis

### 🔴 HIGH RISK: Cognito Therapeutics / MIT Patent Portfolio

**Company:** Cognito Therapeutics (MIT spin-off)  
**Technology:** GENUS (Gamma Entrainment Using Sensory Stimuli) at 40Hz  
**Status:** ACTIVE - FDA Breakthrough Device Designation (2021), Phase 3 trials ongoing

**Key Patents to Monitor:**
- MIT holds foundational patents on 40Hz sensory stimulation for Alzheimer's treatment
- Li-Huei Tsai's lab at MIT pioneered GENUS technology starting in 2016
- Cognito has exclusive licensing rights for therapeutic/medical applications
- Patent filings likely cover: synchronized light/sound at 40Hz, specific device configurations, and therapeutic methods

**Blocking Risk:**
- **HIGH** for any medical/therapeutic claims related to 40Hz stimulation
- **HIGH** for synchronized audio-visual stimulation at gamma frequencies for cognitive disorders
- **MODERATE** for general wellness/cognitive enhancement (depends on claim scope)

**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Position SynSync as **general wellness/cognitive enhancement** (not medical treatment)
- Avoid specific disease claims (Alzheimer's, dementia, etc.)
- Use different frequency combinations or non-synchronized stimulation
- Consider frequencies outside 40Hz (e.g., 38Hz, 42Hz, or multi-frequency approaches)

## 2. Public Domain Analysis

### ✅ FREELY AVAILABLE TECHNOLOGY

**1. Basic Binaural Beats (1839 - Present)**
- Heinrich Wilhelm Dove discovered phenomenon in 1839
- Scientific principle: Two different frequencies → brain perceives difference tone
- **NO PATENT RESTRICTIONS on the fundamental concept**

**2. Frequency Following Response (FFR)**
- Natural brain phenomenon documented since 1934
- Brain entrains to external rhythmic stimuli
- **Scientific fact - not patentable**

**3. Specific Brainwave States**
- Delta (0.1-4 Hz): Deep sleep
- Theta (4-7 Hz): Deep relaxation, meditation
- Alpha (8-13 Hz): Calm focus
- Beta (14-30 Hz): Active thinking
- Gamma (31-100 Hz): High-level cognition
- **Natural phenomena - not patentable**

**4. Expired Patent Methods**
- Basic binaural beat generation (Monroe 1975, 1993, 1994 patents)
- Simple frequency modulation techniques
- Standard audio processing methods

## 5. Patent Strategy Recommendations

### Option A: Defensive Publication (Recommended for Core Tech)

**Strategy:** Publish innovations you want to remain open
- Prevents competitors from patenting
- Maintains freedom to operate
- Low cost

**What to Publish:**
- Novel frequency combinations
- Unique signal processing methods
- Specific application protocols
- User experience innovations

---

### Option B: Provisional Patents (Selective)

**Strategy:** File provisional patents for defensible innovations
- 12-month window to decide on full patent
- Establishes priority date
- Lower cost than full patent

**What to Patent:**
- Novel hardware configurations
- Unique algorithmic approaches
- Specific UX/UI innovations
- Integration methods (if unique)

---

### Option C: Design Around Strategy

**Strategy:** Deliberately avoid infringing patents while achieving similar results

**Design-Around Options:**
1. **Frequency Variations:**
   - Use 38Hz or 42Hz instead of 40Hz
   - Sweep frequencies rather than fixed
   - Multi-frequency harmonics

2. **Delivery Method Variations:**
   - Audio-only (no light sync)
   - Haptic + audio (no visual)
   - Spatial audio techniques

3. **Application Focus:**
   - Wellness/performance (not medical)
   - Gaming/entertainment
   - Meditation/spiritual
