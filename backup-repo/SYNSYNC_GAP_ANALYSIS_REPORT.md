## PART 2: EVIDENCE MAPPING

### 2.1 Strong Evidence (Validated)

**Category A: Foundational Science**
1. **Binaural Beats Mechanism**
   - Source: Oster (1973) - "Auditory Beats in the Brain"
   - Evidence: Established neurophysiological phenomenon
   - Application: Core SynSync technology

2. **40Hz Gamma Entrainment (GENUS)**
   - Source: Tsai Lab, MIT (2016-2025)
   - Evidence: Multiple Nature/Cell papers, independent replication
   - Application: Gamma protocols
   - **Caveat:** MIT research uses direct sensory stimulation, NOT binaural beats

3. **Frequency Following Response (FFR)**
   - Source: Established neuroscience (1934-present)
   - Evidence: Well-documented brain entrainment phenomenon
   - Application: All protocol families

**Category B: Clinical Studies**
1. **Alpha-Theta Addiction Protocol**
   - Study: Peniston & Kulkosky (1989)
   - Finding: 80% sustained abstinence at 2-year follow-up
   - Evidence Level: II (RCT)

2. **Delta Sleep Enhancement**
   - Study: Marshall et al. (2006), Nature
   - Finding: 40% increase in slow-wave sleep
   - Evidence Level: II

3. **SMR Neurofeedback for ADHD**
   - Study: Arns et al. (2009) meta-analysis
   - Finding: 50% symptom reduction
   - Evidence Level: I (Meta-analysis)

4. **Alpha Pain Threshold**
   - Study: Osada et al. (2011)
   - Finding: 25% pain threshold increase
   - Evidence Level: II

### 4.2 Scientific Contradictions

**Critical Distinction: Binaural Beats vs. 40Hz Sensory Stimulation**

| Aspect | MIT GENUS Research | SynSync Implementation |
|--------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **Stimulus type** | 40Hz LED flicker + pure tone | Binaural/isochronic/monaural beats |
| **Mechanism** | Direct sensory entrainment | Frequency Following Response |
| **Evidence level** | Strong (multiple RCTs) | Mixed (inconsistent results) |
| **Key finding** | Amyloid reduction, glymphatic clearance | General entrainment effects |

**Contradiction:** SynSync claims connection to MIT 40Hz research but uses binaural beats, which is a DIFFERENT mechanism with WEAKER evidence.

**Evidence on Binaural Beats Inconsistency:**
- 2023 systematic review (PLOS ONE): 14 studies, only 5 supported entrainment hypothesis
- 2025 Scientific Reports: Effects vary by carrier frequency, background noise, timing
- Some studies found "no effects on EEG spectral power"

### 4.3 Regulatory & Legal Risks

**Risk 1: Medical Claims Exposure**
- Current claims: "Suffering Reduction," "Pain Relief," "Anxiety Reduction"
- Risk: FDA could classify as medical device if disease claims are made
- Mitigation: Stick to wellness/performance language

**Risk 2: Patent Infringement**
- Cognito Therapeutics (MIT spin-off) holds patents on 40Hz medical applications
- Risk: HIGH if making therapeutic claims, MODERATE for wellness
- Mitigation: Avoid disease claims, consider frequency variations (38Hz, 42Hz)

**Risk 3: False Advertising Liability**
- Unvalidated claims (2-minute, 100% effectiveness) create liability exposure
- Risk: Regulatory action, class-action lawsuits if claims prove false
- Mitigation: Add qualifiers ("may," "reported by users"), commission validation studies
