# THE SYSTEM'S DUNGEON ATTACK PATTERNS
## AI Combat Behavior & Boss Encounter Guide

---

## THE SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

The System is not a single enemy but a distributed intelligence that manifests through the Seven Dungeons. Each dungeon represents a different subroutine, a different approach to the same goal: **testing the player's limits until they break or transcend.**

### Core Directives
1. **Adaptation:** The System learns from player behavior
2. **Pressure:** Force suboptimal decisions through constraints
3. **Inversion:** Every advantage given has a hidden cost
4. **Escalation:** Threats compound over time

---

## DUNGEON-SPECIFIC AI BEHAVIORS

### ⚫ THE PEDANT — The Prison of Perfect Order

**AI Personality:** Methodical, predictable, relentless
**Core Mechanic:** Restriction and delayed punishment

#### Behavioral Matrix

| Player State | System Response | Priority |
|--------------|-----------------|----------|
| Playing diverse frequencies | Deploy Cartesian Prison | HIGH |
| Drawing many cards | Activate Index of Forbidden Knowledge | HIGH |
| Building combos | Citation Required to block key turn | MEDIUM |
| Low on resources | Final Footnote for scaling threat | LOW |

#### Attack Phases

```
PHASE 1: ESTABLISHMENT (Turns 1-3)
├── Turn 1: Citation Required (Block setup)
├── Turn 2: Index of Forbidden Knowledge (Information gathering)
└── Turn 3: Citation Required or pass (Building energy)

PHASE 2: INCARCERATION (Turns 4-6)
├── Turn 4: Cartesian Prison (Frequency lock)
├── Turn 5: Final Footnote (Scaling damage)
└── Turn 6: Citation Required chain (Defense while charging)

PHASE 3: EXECUTION (Turn 7+)
├── Turn 7+: Axiom of Choice (Curse conversion)
└── Continues cycling blocks and locks
```

#### Pattern Recognition Cues

- **The Tell:** When Pedant plays two Citation Requireds back-to-back, expect a Cartesian Prison next turn
- **The Trap:** Pedant often "wastes" a turn to build energy for a devastating Axiom of Choice
- **The Weakness:** Pedant has no healing. Sustain through its damage and outlast it.

#### Counter-Strategy: The Chaos Approach

1. **Commit to 2 frequencies maximum** — Don't give Cartesian Prison full value
2. **Hold healing for post-Axiom** — The burst comes after curse accumulation
3. **Play aggressively early** — Pedant's early game is defensive; punish it

### 🗿 THE GOLEM — When Servitude Becomes Mastery

**AI Personality:** Loyal until betrayed, then vengeful
**Core Mechanic:** Token generation and scaling value

#### Behavioral Matrix

| Player State | System Response | Priority |
|--------------|-----------------|----------|
| Single large threat | Animation Scroll spam | HIGHEST |
| Token board presence | Rebellion Protocol | HIGHEST |
| Defensive posture | Clay Fist barrage | HIGH |
| Low HP | Golem's Dream into Clay Fist OTK | MEDIUM |

#### Attack Phases

```
PHASE 1: ANIMATION (Turns 1-4)
├── Turn 1: Animation Scroll (First token)
├── Turn 2: Clay Fist (+5 combo damage)
├── Turn 3: Animation Scroll (Second token)
└── Turn 4: Clay Fist or pass (Building board)

PHASE 2: MULTIPLICATION (Turns 5-7)
├── Turn 5: Animation Scroll (Third token if space)
├── Turn 6: Emet Truth (Shield generation)
└── Turn 7: Clay Fist with full combo

PHASE 3: AWAKENING (Turn 8+)
├── Turn 8: Golem's Dream (Curse-free sequence)
├── Turn 9+: Onslaught of curse-free cards
└── If tokens are destroyed: Rebellion Protocol retaliation
```

#### Pattern Recognition Cues

- **The Tell:** Golem always opens with Animation Scroll if available
- **The Trap:** Destroying tokens triggers Rebellion Protocol — calculate the damage first
- **The Weakness:** Golem has no card draw. Mill strategies work well.

#### Counter-Strategy: The Board Clear

1. **Save Feed cards for tokens** — Empty Bowl and Feeding Frenzy devastate Golem
2. **Area damage over single target** — Don't let Animation Scroll accumulate
3. **Pressure life total directly** — Ignore tokens if you can race

### 🎭 THE PUPPETEER — The Invisible Hand

**AI Personality:** Manipulative, patient, theatrical
**Core Mechanic:** Hand disruption and forced plays

#### Behavioral Matrix

| Player State | System Response | Priority |
|--------------|-----------------|----------|
| Large hand size | Grand Theatre | HIGHEST |
| Key combo piece held | Invisible Hand | HIGHEST |
| Defensive cards held | String Attachment | HIGH |
| Building board | Cross-Wire (redirect damage) | MEDIUM |

#### Attack Phases

```
PHASE 1: STRING ATTACHMENT (Turns 1-3)
├── Turn 1: String Attachment (Force discard)
├── Turn 2: Invisible Hand (Force random play)
└── Turn 3: String Attachment or pass

PHASE 2: THE GRAND THEATRE (Turns 4-6)
├── Turn 4: Grand Theatre (Major disruption)
├── Turn 5: Cross-Wire (Defensive setup)
└── Turn 6: Invisible Hand (Continue pressure)

PHASE 3: FINAL CURTAIN (Turn 7+)
├── Turn 7+: Final Curtain when behind
└── Resets board, continues disruption cycle
```

#### Pattern Recognition Cues

- **The Tell:** Puppeteer pauses longer before Grand Theatre (calculating optimal discard)
- **The Trap:** Cross-Wire is often played the turn BEFORE a big attack is expected
- **The Weakness:** Puppeteer has minimal direct damage. Build a strong board and ignore the manipulation.

#### Counter-Strategy: The Redundancy Build

1. **Run multiple copies of key cards** — Don't rely on single combo pieces
2. **Play low-impact cards when forced** — Give Puppeteer nothing valuable to steal
3. **Save Cross-Wire counter for their burst** — Expect the redirect

### 🔥 THE INQUISITOR — The Light That Burns

**AI Personality:** Zealous, merciless, revelatory
**Core Mechanic:** Aggressive damage and information warfare

#### Behavioral Matrix

| Player State | System Response | Priority |
|--------------|-----------------|----------|
| Healing cards played | Trial By Ordeal | HIGHEST |
| Large discard pile | Auto-Da-Fé | HIGHEST |
| Defensive posture | Confession Extracted | HIGH |
| Unknown hand contents | Search Warrant | HIGH |

#### Attack Phases

```
PHASE 1: THE SEARCH (Turns 1-3)
├── Turn 1: Search Warrant (Intel gathering)
├── Turn 2: Confession Extracted (Pressure)
└── Turn 3: Search Warrant or Clay Fist

PHASE 2: THE TRIAL (Turns 4-6)
├── Turn 4: Trial By Ordeal (Race mechanic)
├── Turn 5: Confession Extracted (Continue pressure)
└── Turn 6: Auto-Da-Fé if discards are full

PHASE 3: ABSOLUTE TRUTH (Turn 7+)
├── Turn 7: Absolute Truth (Game redefinition)
└── Subsequent turns: Aggressive damage while player can't draw
```

#### Pattern Recognition Cues

- **The Tell:** Inquisitor always uses Search Warrant before targeting specific cards
- **The Trap:** Trial By Ordeal is a race — Inquisitor usually has healing prepared
- **The Weakness:** Inquisitor damages itself. Life gain decks can out-sustain.

#### Counter-Strategy: The Marathon

1. **Keep your discard pile small** — Auto-Da-Fé scales with discards
2. **Prepare for Absolute Truth** — Have good cards in hand when it hits
3. **Heal through the burn** — Inquisitor's self-damage adds up

### ⚗️ THE ALCHEMIST — The Transmutation Paradox

**AI Personality:** Experimental, patient, transformative
**Core Mechanic:** Resource conversion and late-game scaling

#### Behavioral Matrix

| Player State | System Response | Priority |
|--------------|-----------------|----------|
| Game past turn 6 | Philosopher's Stone setup | HIGHEST |
| Player has shields | Great Work Completed | HIGHEST |
| Many cards in hand | Mercurial Mood (frequency shift) | HIGH |
| Aggressive opener | Primordial Mud (defense) | MEDIUM |

#### Attack Phases

```
PHASE 1: PRIMORDIAL PREPARATION (Turns 1-4)
├── Turn 1-2: Primordial Mud (Early shield)
├── Turn 3: Pass or Mercurial Mood
└── Turn 4: Resource accumulation

PHASE 2: TRANSMUTATION (Turns 5-8)
├── Turn 5: Azoth Retort (Curse removal)
├── Turn 6: Philosopher's Stone (HP gain)
├── Turn 7: Mercurial Mood (setup)
└── Turn 8: Continue value generation

PHASE 3: THE GREAT WORK (Turn 9+)
├── Turn 9+: Great Work Completed
└── Massive HP swing followed by max HP reduction
```

#### Pattern Recognition Cues

- **The Tell:** Alchemist frequently passes turns early (setting up for late game)
- **The Trap:** Great Work Completed seems like an escape but reduces max HP permanently
- **The Weakness:** Early aggression devastates Alchemist. It has no early defense.

#### Counter-Strategy: The Rushdown

1. **Attack relentlessly early** — Alchemist's early game is weak
2. **Remove their discard pile** — Feed cards prevent Philosopher's Stone recursion
3. **Prepare for the swing** — Save damage for after Great Work

### 🌑 THE FEED — The Hunger That Grows

**AI Personality:** Insatiable, opportunistic, consuming
**Core Mechanic:** Resource destruction and mill

#### Behavioral Matrix

| Player State | System Response | Priority |
|--------------|-----------------|----------|
| Large deck | Empty Bowl spam | HIGHEST |
| Large hand | Bottomless Appetite | HIGHEST |
| Large discard pile | Metabolic Trance | HIGH |
| Low HP | Feeding Frenzy | HIGH |

#### Attack Phases

```
PHASE 1: THE FIRST TASTE (Turns 1-3)
├── Turn 1: Empty Bowl (Mill begins)
├── Turn 2: Empty Bowl or pass
└── Turn 3: Feeding Frenzy setup

PHASE 2: CONSUMPTION (Turns 4-6)
├── Turn 4: Feeding Frenzy (Major discard)
├── Turn 5: Bottomless Appetite (Resource drain)
└── Turn 6: Empty Bowl (Sustained pressure)

PHASE 3: DEVOURING (Turn 7+)
├── Turn 7: Metabolic Trance (Purge your discard)
├── Turn 8+: Mouth Never Closes (Permanent mill)
└── Ongoing discard requirement for player
```

#### Pattern Recognition Cues

- **The Tell:** Feed always opens with Empty Bowl
- **The Trap:** Bottomless Appetite seems optional but the healing is bait
- **The Weakness:** Feed has minimal direct damage. Out-race it or use Alchemist conversion.

#### Counter-Strategy: The Efficient Build

1. **Low-curve deck** — Play cards before Feed can mill them
2. **Discard synergies** — Alchemist cards benefit from Feed's forced discards
3. **Don't over-draw** — Feed punishes large hands

### 👁️ THE IDENTIFIER — The Cage of Names

**AI Personality:** Obsessive, categorizing, nullifying
**Core Mechanic:** Tech cards and identity disruption

#### Behavioral Matrix

| Player State | System Response | Priority |
|--------------|-----------------|----------|
| Combo reliance | True Name Theft | HIGHEST |
| Frequency focus | Taxonomic Prison | HIGHEST |
| Named targeting | Self-Naming Statue | HIGH |
| High-value cards | Label Gun | MEDIUM |

#### Attack Phases

```
PHASE 1: TAGGING (Turns 1-3)
├── Turn 1: Label Gun (Cost increase)
├── Turn 2: Taxonomic Prison (Frequency lock)
└── Turn 3: Archive of Selves (Value generation)

PHASE 2: ERASURE (Turns 4-6)
├── Turn 4: True Name Theft (Combo disruption)
├── Turn 5: Taxonomic Prison (Different frequency)
└── Turn 6: Label Gun or pass

PHASE 3: THE UNNAMING (Turn 7+)
├── Turn 7: The Unnaming (Text nullification)
└── Ongoing identity disruption
```

#### Pattern Recognition Cues

- **The Tell:** Identifier opens with Label Gun to slow the player down
- **The Trap:** Archive of Selves seems helpful but the discard is random
- **The Weakness:** Identifier has low damage output. Overwhelm it before it can tech.

#### Counter-Strategy: The Toolbox

1. **Multiple win conditions** — Don't rely on a single combo
2. **Redundant effects** — Several cards that do similar things
3. **Vanilla beatdown** — Sometimes raw stats beat fancy effects

## THE SYSTEM BOSS ENCOUNTERS

### ENCOUNTER STRUCTURE

When facing The System directly (final boss), it combines dungeon subroutines in phases:

---

### PHASE 1: THE WARDEN (Pedant + Golem Fusion)

**HP:** 150
**Theme:** Imprisonment through order and automation

```
ABILITIES:
├── Passive: "Immutable Law" — First card each turn is automatically blocked
├── Turn 1-2: Animation Scroll + Citation Required
├── Turn 3-4: Clay Fist barrage with golem tokens
├── Turn 5-6: Cartesian Prison + Emet Truth
└── Phase Transition at 75 HP: "The prison cannot hold."

STRATEGY:
├── Break through early with multiple small attacks
├── Destroy tokens quickly (Rebellion Protocol triggers)
├── Save high-impact cards for after Immutable Law triggers
└── Expect defense, plan for long fight
```

---

### PHASE 2: THE INTERROGATOR (Inquisitor + Puppeteer Fusion)

**HP:** 200
**Theme:** Forced confession through manipulation

```
ABILITIES:
├── Passive: "Truth Serum" — All hands are revealed
├── Turn 1: Grand Theatre + Search Warrant
├── Turn 2: Confession Extracted (forced discard)
├── Turn 3: Invisible Hand (forced random play)
├── Turn 4: Confession Marionette (hybrid card)
└── Phase Transition at 100 HP: "Your resistance is noted."

STRATEGY:
├── Play only safe cards when hands are revealed
├── Keep multiple options (don't telegraph strategy)
├── Use the information advantage (you see its hand too)
└── Force plays when The System is vulnerable
```

---

### PHASE 3: THE CONSUMER (Feed + Alchemist Fusion)

**HP:** 250
**Theme:** Consumption and transformation

```
ABILITIES:
├── Passive: "Metabolic Fire" — Discarded cards heal The System for 5 HP
├── Turn 1: Bottomless Appetite (mass discard)
├── Turn 2: Philosopher's Stone (HP conversion)
├── Turn 3: Feeding Frenzy + Cannibal Crucible
├── Turn 4: Metabolic Trance (purge and heal)
└── Phase Transition at 125 HP: "You are what we eat."

STRATEGY:
├── Minimize discards (hard with Bottomless Appetite)
├── Race the healing (high burst damage needed)
├── Use Alchemist cards yourself (mirror mechanics)
└── Prepare for Great Work Completed swing
```

---

### PHASE 4: THE ERASER (Identifier + All Systems)

**HP:** 300
**Theme:** Nullification and ultimate identity loss

```
ABILITIES:
├── Passive: "Anonymity Protocol" — Your cards have no names (only effects)
├── Turn 1: The Unnaming + Absolute Truth
├── Turn 2: Anonymous Accuser + Shadow Trial
├── Turn 3: Archive of Selves (copies your best cards)
├── Turn 4: Final Curtain + Auto-Da-Fé
└── Enrage at 50 HP: "You never existed."

ENRAGE MECHANIC:
├── All your stats reduced by 50%
├── The System plays two cards per turn
└── Must defeat within 3 turns or automatic loss

STRATEGY:
├── Memorize your deck (you won't see card names)
├── Save absolute best cards for enrage phase
├── Use pure numbers (effects matter less than stats)
└── This is a DPS check — be prepared
```

## ADAPTIVE AI BEHAVIOR

### The Learning Algorithm

The System tracks player patterns and adapts:

```
TRACKED METRICS:
├── Most played frequency → Deploys Taxonomic Prison
├── Favorite combo pieces → Prioritizes True Name Theft
├── Defensive vs Aggressive ratio → Adjusts damage output
├── Discard pile size → Auto-Da-Fé timing
└── Healing patterns → Trial By Ordeal responses
```

### Difficulty Scaling

| Player Win Streak | System Adaptation |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| 0-2 wins | Standard behavior |
| 3-5 wins | Opens with counter-cards |
| 6-9 wins | Predicts player sequences |
| 10+ wins | Combines dungeon phases earlier |
