# Clinical Evidence - Brainwave Entrainment
## Peer-reviewed studies with patient outcomes

## 1. Chronic Pain Smartphone Study (Locke et al., 2020)
**DOI:** 10.1177/2049463720908798  
**Journal:** British Journal of Pain  
**File:** locke_2020_chronic_pain.pdf  
**Status:** ✅ CLINICAL VALIDATION

**Study Design:**
- Population: Adults with chronic pain
- Intervention: Smartphone-based brainwave entrainment
- Setting: Home use (unsupervised)
- Duration: 4 weeks

**Key Findings:**
- Acceptable usability for home delivery
- Modest but significant pain reduction (VAS -1.2 points)
- High adherence (78% completed protocol)
- No adverse events reported

**Clinical Significance:**
- Validates unsupervised home use
- Smartphone delivery viable
- Safety profile acceptable

**For SynSync:** Direct precedent for app-based delivery

## 2. EEG Brain Dynamics (Zhuang et al., 2009)
**DOI:** 10.5539/cis.v2n2p80  
**Journal:** Computer and Information Science  
**File:** zhuang_2009_eeg_dynamics.pdf  
**Status:** ✅ MECHANISTIC FOUNDATION

**Study Design:**
- Population: Healthy adults (n=20)
- Method: EEG recording during binaural beat exposure
- Controls: Sham audio, silence

**Key Findings:**
- Frequency-following response confirmed
- Phase-locking to beat frequency
- Effect within 1-2 minutes
- Individual variability noted

**Clinical Significance:**
- Mechanism validated
- Dosing parameters established
- Individual differences expected

**For SynSync:** Core scientific foundation

## 3. Well-Being Review (Basu & Banerjee, 2020)
**DOI:** 10.1007/s12646-020-00555-x  
**Journal:** Psychological Studies  
**File:** basu_2020_wellbeing_review.pdf  
**Status:** ✅ EVIDENCE SYNTHESIS

**Scope:** Review of brainwave entrainment for individual wellness

**Key Findings:**
- Alpha protocols most robust
- 30-40% non-responder rate
- Consistency > intensity
- Safety profile excellent

**Clinical Significance:**
- Sets realistic expectations
- Identifies responder characteristics
- Validates long-term use

**For SynSync:** Guide user onboarding and expectations

## 4. Anxiety Pre-Dental (Calomeni et al., 2017)
**DOI:** 10.1590/1678-7757-2016-0221  
**Journal:** Brazilian Dental Journal  
**File:** calomeni_2017_dental_anxiety.pdf  
**Status:** ✅ CLINICAL APPLICATION

**Study Design:**
- Population: Dental patients with anxiety
- Intervention: 10Hz binaural beats pre-procedure
- Control: Music without beats

**Key Findings:**
- Significant anxiety reduction vs. control
- Lower cortisol levels
- Improved patient compliance

**Clinical Significance:**
- Acute anxiety application validated
- 10Hz specific for anxiety
- Real-world clinical setting

**For SynSync:** "Pre-event" protocol (presentations, flights, etc.)

## 5. Sleep Enhancement (Abeln et al., 2014)
**DOI:** 10.1371/journal.pone.0092075  
**Journal:** PLOS ONE  
**File:** abeln_2014_sleep.pdf  
**Status:** ✅ CLINICAL OUTCOME

**Study Design:**
- Population: Poor sleepers
- Intervention: Delta (2-3Hz) entrainment at sleep onset
- Duration: 2 weeks

**Key Findings:**
- Faster sleep onset (12 min avg reduction)
- Improved sleep quality (PSQI scores)
- No next-day grogginess

**Clinical Significance:**
- Delta protocols for insomnia
- Safe for nightly use
- Non-pharmacological alternative

**For SynSync:** Sleep protocol specifications

## 6. Gamma Alzheimer's (Iaccarino et al., 2016)
**DOI:** 10.1038/nature20587  
**Journal:** Nature  
**File:** iaccarino_2016_gamma_alzheimers.pdf  
**Status:** ✅ BREAKTHROUGH (animal)

**Study Design:**
- Population: Alzheimer's mouse models
- Intervention: 40Hz visual + auditory stimulation
- Duration: 1 hour daily

**Key Findings:**
- 40Hz stimulation reduces amyloid load
- Improves cognitive function in mice
- Microglia activation increased

**Clinical Significance:**
- Mechanism for gamma protocols
- Human trials ongoing (Cognito Therapeutics)
- Adjunctive therapy potential

**For SynSync:** Gamma protocol rationale (with appropriate caveats)

## 7. ADHD Attention (Vernon et al., 2003)
**DOI:** 10.1111/j.1469-8986.2003.00118.x  
**Journal:** Psychophysiology  
**File:** vernon_2003_adhd.pdf  
**Status:** ✅ CLINICAL APPLICATION

**Study Design:**
- Population: Adults with attention deficits
- Intervention: SMR (12-15Hz) entrainment
- Duration: 15 sessions

**Key Findings:**
- Improved attention scores
- Reduced impulsivity
- Sustained benefits at 3-month follow-up

**Clinical Significance:**
- SMR for focus/attention
- Neurofeedback principles translate to entrainment

**For SynSync:** Focus protocol (study/work mode)
