## PHASE 1: LITERATURE AUDIT (4 Papers)

### Paper 1: Tinston et al. (2025) — MOST RECENT
**Title:** Forty-hertz sensory entrainment impedes kindling epileptogenesis and reduces amyloid pathology in an Alzheimer disease mouse model

**DOI:** 10.1111/epi.18222  
**PMID:** 39737719  
**Journal:** Epilepsia (2025 Mar;66(3):886-898)  
**Authors:** Jennifer Tinston, Matthew R Hudson, Anna Harutyunyan, Zhibin Chen, Nigel C Jones  
**Institution:** Monash University, Melbourne

**Sample:** 5xFAD mice + wild-type littermates (n=7-11/group)

**Primary Findings:**
- 40Hz audiovisual stimulation reduced behavioral severity of first seizure (OR=4.04, p=.02)
- Delayed epileptogenesis (IRR=0.82, p=.01) — increased stimulations needed to reach kindling endpoint
- ~50% reduction in amyloid pathology in 5xFAD mice receiving stimulation
- Astrocyte and microglia markers upregulated in both genotypes
- Antiepileptogenic effects observed even in WT mice (suggests mechanism beyond Aβ reduction)

**Effect Sizes:** OR=4.04 (first seizure severity), IRR=0.82 (kindling delay)

**Methodological Notes:**
- 1h/day stimulation, began 2 weeks before kindling, continued throughout
- Amygdala kindling model (electrical stimulation)
- Postmortem IHC for Aβ, astrocyte, microglia morphology

**Confidence:** 95% (well-powered, clear stats, recent)

### Paper 2: Yang & Lai (2023) — NEGATIVE RESULT
**Title:** Prolonged 40-Hz Flickering Light Failed to Reduce Amyloid Load in 5xFAD Mice

**DOI:** 10.1523/ENEURO.0189-23.2023  
**PMID:** 37550065  
**Journal:** eNeuro (2023 Aug;10(8))  
**Authors:** Chieh-Hsiang Yang, Ming-Yi Lai  
**Institution:** National Health Research Institutes, Taiwan

**Sample:** 5xFAD mice (n=4/group), 33-week-old, 5-week treatment

**Primary Findings:**
- **NO EFFECT:** 40Hz flickering light for 5 weeks did NOT reduce Aβ load in visual cortex or hippocampus
- **NO EFFECT:** No increase in microglial size compared to continuous light or 24Hz/80Hz
- Contradicts previous shorter-term studies (1h or 7 days) that showed Aβ reduction

**Methodological Notes:**
- Longer treatment period (5 weeks) vs previous studies (1h-7 days)
- Compared 24Hz, 40Hz, 80Hz, continuous light
- Only visual stimulation (no audio)
- Small sample size (n=4/group)

**Confidence:** 85% (small n, but clear negative result)

**VESPER FLAG:** This is a failed replication — critical for VESPER to assess

### Paper 3: Soula et al. (2023) — NEGATIVE RESULT
**Title:** 40Hz visual stimulation does not engage microglia or reduce amyloid-β in APP/PS1 or 5xFAD mice

**DOI:** 10.1038/s41593-023-01290-3  
**PMID:** 36879142  
**Journal:** Nature Neuroscience (2023 Apr;26(4):570-580)  
**Authors:** Mariela C. Soula, Alessio P. Buccino, et al.  
**Institution:** University of Oslo, Norway

**Sample:** APP/PS1 and 5xFAD mice, multi-site silicon probe recording

**Primary Findings:**
- **NO ENGAGEMENT:** 40Hz flickering did NOT engage native gamma oscillations in visual cortex, entorhinal cortex, or hippocampus
- **WEAK RESPONSE:** Spike responses in hippocampus were weak — 40Hz light does not effectively entrain deep structures
- **NO EFFECT:** No reliable changes in plaque count, microglia morphology, or Aβ 40/42 levels
- **AVERSION:** Mice avoided 40Hz flickering (associated with elevated cholinergic activity)

**Methodological Notes:**
- Acute stimulation (10 min baseline + 1h stimulation)
- Multi-site silicon probe recording (visual, entorhinal, hippocampus)
- In vivo two-photon imaging
- ELISA for Aβ quantification

**Confidence:** 95% (Nature journal, rigorous methods, multiple approaches)

**VESPER FLAG:** Major negative finding — challenges fundamental mechanism assumptions

### Paper 4: Chan et al. (2022) — HUMAN PILOT (POSITIVE)
**Title:** Gamma frequency sensory stimulation in mild probable Alzheimer's dementia patients: Results of feasibility and pilot studies

**DOI:** 10.1371/journal.pone.0278412  
**PMID:** 36454969  
**Journal:** PLoS One (2022)  
**Authors:** Diane Chan, Hannah J Suh, et al. (Li-Huei Tsai senior author)  
**Institution:** MIT, Mass General, Broad Institute

**Sample:** 
- Phase 1: 25 cognitively normal, 16 mild AD, 2 epilepsy (intracranial)
- Phase 2A: 15 mild AD (randomized, single-blind, placebo-controlled)

**Primary Findings:**
- **SAFETY:** Well-tolerated, no serious adverse events
- **ENTRAINMENT:** EEG showed 40Hz entrainment in cortical and subcortical regions (hippocampus, amygdala, insula)
- **STRUCTURAL:** Less ventricular dilation and hippocampal atrophy at 3 months (p=0.024)
- **FUNCTIONAL:** Increased default mode network connectivity (p<0.05 FWE-corrected)
- **COGNITIVE:** Better face-name association delayed recall (trend)
- **ACTIVITY:** Improved daily activity rhythmicity (p=0.007 at Month 4)

**Clinical Trial IDs:** NCT04042922 (Phase 1), NCT04055376 (Phase 2A)

**Confidence:** 90% (human data, randomized controlled, but small n, short follow-up)

**Conflict of Interest:** Li-Huei Tsai is co-founder of Cognito Therapeutics (commercializing GENUS)

| Tinston | 2025 | 5xFAD mice | ✅ Positive | 50% Aβ reduction, antiepileptogenic |

| Soula et al. | 2023 | APP/PS1, 5xFAD | ❌ Negative | No entrainment of deep structures, no microglia activation |
| Chan et al. | 2022 | Humans (mild AD) | ✅ Positive | Structural + functional improvements, safe |

**PATTERN:** Mouse studies are MIXED (2 positive, 2 negative). Human pilot is positive but small.

**CRITICAL QUESTION:** Why the discrepancy between mouse studies?
- Stimulation duration (acute vs chronic)?
- Audiovisual combined vs visual-only?
- Mouse age/strain differences?
- Publication bias (negative results harder to publish)?
