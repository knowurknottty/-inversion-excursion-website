## Redaction Mechanics

### Redaction Percentage Tiers
- **0-30%** (Low): Most content visible, minimal power
- **31-50%** (Medium): Key details hidden, moderate power
- **51-70%** (High): Significant redaction, high power
- **71-90%** (Severe): Most content hidden, very high power
- **91-100%** (Extreme): Nearly complete blackout, maximum power

### Redaction & Power Relationship
Higher redaction = Higher mystery = Higher power potential
However, some skills require specific revealed content to activate.

## Lore Integration

### The Forger's Guild
Documents are created by the **Forgers of the Black Archive**—an ancient order dedicated to preserving truth through controlled revelation. Each document is both a weapon and a burden.

### The Redaction Paradox
The Forgers discovered that partial truth is more powerful than full revelation. The black bars don't hide—they amplify curiosity, and curiosity is the engine of awakening.

### The Heinousness Principle
Documents of higher heinousness carry spiritual weight. Prolonged exposure to a heinousness 10 document can cause:
- Paranoia (gameplay debuff)
- Insight (special vision modes)
- Corruption (irreversible alignment shift)

## Design Philosophy

> *"Every redaction is a question. Every question is a weapon. Every weapon demands a wielder strong enough to bear the truth."*
> — The First Forger

These documents exist in the space between conspiracy and fact, between paranoia and pattern recognition. They are fiction wearing the mask of evidence, and that mask is the point.

The references are deliberate echoes of real-world mysteries—Roswell, MKULTRA, Epstein, 9/11 questions, UFO disclosure—reframed as collectible game artifacts. The player who collects them is playing a game within a game: the game of "what if the wildest theories were documented fact?"
