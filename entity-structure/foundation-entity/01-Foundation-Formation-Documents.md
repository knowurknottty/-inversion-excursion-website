# PART A: MARSHALL ISLANDS NON-PROFIT CORPORATION OPTION

## ARTICLES OF INCORPORATION

### ARTICLE I: NAME

The name of the corporation is:

**SYNCHRONICITY FOUNDATION, INC.**

### ARTICLE II: DURATION

The corporation shall have perpetual existence.

### ARTICLE III: PURPOSES

#### 3.1 Primary Purpose

The Foundation is organized exclusively for charitable, educational, and scientific purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the U.S. Internal Revenue Code, specifically to:

(a) **Combat Human Trafficking:** Support organizations dedicated to preventing human trafficking, rescuing victims, and providing survivor services, with initial focus on Polaris Project;

(b) **Technology Accessibility:** Promote accessible wellness technology for underserved and vulnerable populations;

(c) **Digital Rights Education:** Educate the public about privacy-preserving technology, decentralized systems, and digital sovereignty;

(d) **Scientific Research:** Fund research in neuroscience, brainwave entrainment, and related fields for public benefit;

(e) **Economic Empowerment:** Support financial literacy and economic opportunity programs for marginalized communities.

#### 3.2 Specific Powers

The Foundation shall have all powers necessary to accomplish its purposes, including:
- Receiving and administering charitable contributions;
- Making grants to qualified charitable organizations;
- Conducting educational programs;
- Publishing research and educational materials;
- Collaborating with other charitable organizations;
- Engaging in advocacy consistent with 501(c)(3) limitations.

#### 3.3 Prohibited Activities

The Foundation shall NOT:
(a) Engage in lobbying beyond permitted 501(c)(3) limits;
(b) Support or oppose political candidates;
(c) Distribute assets to private individuals except as reasonable compensation;
(d) Operate for the benefit of private interests;
(e) Conduct unrelated business activities beyond permitted limits.

### ARTICLE IV: NON-PROFIT AND NON-STOCK STRUCTURE

(a) The Foundation is a non-stock corporation;
(b) No shares or ownership interests shall be issued;
(c) No part of net earnings shall inure to the benefit of any private shareholder or individual;
(d) Upon dissolution, assets shall be distributed for charitable purposes.

### ARTICLE V: MEMBERSHIP

The Foundation shall have no members.

### ARTICLE VI: BOARD OF DIRECTORS

#### 6.1 Number and Qualification

The Foundation shall be governed by a Board of Directors consisting of not less than three (3) and not more than nine (9) directors.

#### 6.2 Initial Directors

The initial directors are:
- [Director Name 1], [Title/Occupation]
- [Director Name 2], [Title/Occupation]
- [Director Name 3], [Title/Occupation]

#### 6.3 Director Qualifications

Directors shall have expertise in:
- Nonprofit governance and management;
- Human rights and anti-trafficking work;
- Technology and blockchain;
- Finance and investments.

#### 6.4 Terms

Directors serve staggered three-year terms, with one-third elected annually.

### ARTICLE VII: REGISTERED AGENT AND OFFICE

**Registered Agent:** [Marshall Islands Trust Company Name]
**Address:** [Address], Majuro, Marshall Islands MH 96960

### ARTICLE VIII: DISSOLUTION

Upon dissolution or winding up:
(a) All liabilities and obligations shall be paid;
(b) Remaining assets shall be distributed to:
   - Polaris Project (primary beneficiary); or
   - Other 501(c)(3) organizations with similar purposes;
(c) No assets shall be distributed to private individuals;
(d) Distribution shall comply with Marshall Islands law and U.S. tax requirements.

### ARTICLE IX: ASSET DEDICATION

All assets of the Foundation are irrevocably dedicated to charitable purposes.

## BYLAWS

### ARTICLE I: BOARD OF DIRECTORS

#### 1.1 Powers

The Board shall have all powers necessary to govern the Foundation, including:
- Setting strategic direction;
- Approving grants and expenditures;
- Hiring and supervising staff;
- Managing investments;
- Ensuring legal and ethical compliance.

#### 1.2 Meetings

(a) **Annual Meeting:** Held within 90 days of fiscal year end;
(b) **Regular Meetings:** Minimum quarterly;
(c) **Special Meetings:** Called by Chair or majority of directors;
(d) **Notice:** 7 days for regular meetings, 48 hours for special meetings;
(e) **Quorum:** Majority of directors;
(f) **Voting:** Majority of directors present, except as otherwise specified.

#### 1.3 Committees

The Board may establish:
(a) **Grant Committee:** Reviews and recommends grants;
(b) **Finance Committee:** Oversees investments and budget;
(c) **Audit Committee:** Oversees audits and compliance;
(d) **Governance Committee:** Manages board development.

### ARTICLE II: OFFICERS

#### 2.1 Officers

The officers shall be:
- Chair of the Board
- Vice Chair
- Secretary
- Treasurer

#### 2.2 Duties

**Chair:** Presides at meetings, chief executive officer;
**Vice Chair:** Performs Chair duties in absence;
**Secretary:** Maintains records, minutes, corporate books;
**Treasurer:** Manages finances, prepares reports.

The Foundation may receive donations from:

- Bequests and planned gifts;
- Cryptocurrency and digital assets.

#### 3.2 Grant Making

The Foundation's primary grantee shall be Polaris Project, receiving not less than 90% of annual distributions unless changed by 75% Board vote.

**(b) Grant Criteria:**
All grants must support the Foundation's charitable purposes;

## CONFLICT OF INTEREST POLICY

### Article I: Purpose

This policy protects the Foundation's interests when contemplating transactions or arrangements that might benefit private interests.

### Article II: Definitions

**Conflict of Interest:** Any financial interest, business relationship, or personal relationship that could affect a director's judgment.

**Financial Interest:** Ownership, investment, compensation, or gift arrangements.

### Article III: Procedures

1. **Annual Disclosures:** All directors complete annual conflict disclosure statements;
2. **Meeting Disclosures:** Directors disclose conflicts at meetings where conflicted matters arise;
3. **Recusal:** Conflicted directors leave meeting during discussion and vote;
4. **Documentation:** Minutes record disclosure, recusal, and Board decision;
5. **Review:** Independent directors review and approve related party transactions.

### Article IV: DAO Relationship

The Foundation acknowledges its relationship with Synchronicity DAO LLC:
- DAO is the primary donor;
- Some directors may have DAO affiliations;
- Such relationships are disclosed but do not automatically disqualify;
- Foundation maintains independent governance.

# PART B: SWISS VEREIN OPTION

## ALTERNATIVE STRUCTURE: SWISS ASSOCIATION (VEREIN)

### Why Switzerland?

1. **Prestige and Credibility:** Swiss nonprofit status carries global recognition;
2. **Political Neutrality:** Switzerland's neutral status benefits international operations;
3. **Crypto-Friendly:** Clear regulatory framework for cryptocurrency donations;
4. **Tax Efficiency:** Potential tax exemptions for qualifying activities;
5. **Banking Access:** Access to sophisticated banking and custody services.

### Formation Requirements

**Articles of Association (Statuten)** must specify:
- Name and registered office;
- Purpose (must be non-profit);
- Organizational structure;
- Membership (if any) or self-governing structure;
- Asset dedication to charitable purposes.

**Registration:**
- File with Commercial Register;
- Minimum capital: CHF 0 (no minimum);
- Notarization required;
- Estimated costs: CHF 5,000-15,000.

**Tax Exemption:**
- Apply for tax exemption with cantonal tax authority;
- Must demonstrate exclusive charitable purposes;
- Approval typically within 3-6 months;
- Requires ongoing compliance and reporting.

### Swiss Verein Bylaws (Key Provisions)

#### Article 1: Name and Seat

**Name:** Synchronicity Foundation (Swiss Verein)
**Seat:** [City], Switzerland

#### Article 2: Purpose

The Verein is organized exclusively for charitable purposes, including:
- Support for human rights organizations;
- Promotion of technology accessibility;
- Scientific research funding;
- Public education.

The Verein operates without commercial purpose and does not seek profit.

#### Article 3: Membership

The Verein has no members.
The Verein is self-governing through its Board.

#### Article 4: Governance

**General Assembly:** Composed of Board members;
**Board:** Minimum 3 members, manages Verein affairs;
**Auditor:** Optional for small Vereins, required if certain thresholds met.

#### Article 5: Asset Dedication

All assets are irrevocably dedicated to charitable purposes.
Upon dissolution, assets transferred to Swiss charitable organization with similar purposes.

# PART C: SWISS FOUNDATION OPTION

## SWISS FOUNDATION (STIFTUNG) STRUCTURE

### Characteristics

- Independent legal entity;
- No members or shareholders;
- Purpose permanently defined in foundation deed;
- Board has fiduciary duties to foundation purpose;
- High level of asset protection.

### Formation

1. **Foundation Deed:** Notarized deed specifying purpose, assets, governance;
2. **Registration:** Entry in Commercial Register;
3. **Supervision:** Subject to state foundation supervision (depending on canton);
4. **Minimum Assets:** No statutory minimum, but practical minimum CHF 50,000-100,000.

### Governance

**Foundation Board:** Manages foundation affairs;
**Auditor:** Required;
**Supervisory Authority:** Cantonal foundation supervisory authority.

## COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK

### U.S. Tax Compliance (for 501(c)(3) qualification)

1. **Public Charity Status:**
   - Normally receive substantial support from public or governmental units;
   - Synchronicity DAO LLC is considered a "public" supporter if broadly held.

2. **Private Foundation Avoidance:**
   - Must meet public support test (normally >1/3 public support);
   - Diversify funding sources;
   - Maintain active fundraising.

3. **Form 990 Filing:**
   - Annual information return;
   - Public disclosure required;
   - Schedule B may disclose major donors.

4. **Unrelated Business Income:**
   - Monitor for UBI that could trigger tax;
   - Cryptocurrency staking income analysis needed.

### Anti-Terrorism Compliance

1. **OFAC Screening:**
   - Screen all grantees against sanctions lists;
   - Verify no prohibited transactions;
   - Document screening procedures.

2. **Grantee Due Diligence:**
   - Verify 501(c)(3) status;
   - Review financials and governance;
   - Monitor for red flags.
