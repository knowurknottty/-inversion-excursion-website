(a) **Smart Contract Address(es):** [Primary Governance Contract Address(es)]
(b) **Blockchain Network:** [Ethereum/Solana/etc.]
(c) **Governance Token:** [Token Contract Address]
(d) **Voting Mechanism:** Token-weighted quadratic voting with delegation

**Section 5.2 - Smart Contract Governance Recognition**

The Company hereby adopts the following principles of smart contract governance pursuant to Wyoming law:

1. **Smart Contract Supremacy:** To the extent permitted by Wyoming law, the rights, obligations, and governance of Members shall be determined by the smart contracts governing the DAO, as specified in the Operating Agreement.

2. **Amendment Protocol:** Changes to governance smart contracts require:
   - Minimum 14-day notice period
   - Supermajority vote of 66% of voting power
   - Execution through approved multi-signature wallet or governance contract

3. **Member Rights:** Members retain all rights under Wyoming law, including the right to petition a court of competent jurisdiction if smart contract governance violates applicable law or the Operating Agreement.

### ARTICLE VI: MANAGEMENT STRUCTURE

**Section 6.1 - Member-Managed with Delegated Authority**

The Company is member-managed, with day-to-day operational authority delegated to:
- The smart contract governance system for protocol-level decisions
- Service Providers engaged through DAO vote for operational execution
- A Stewardship Council (as defined in Operating Agreement) for legal compliance and emergency actions

**Section 6.2 - Limitations on Manager Authority**

Notwithstanding any delegation, the following actions require direct DAO vote:
1. Amendment to the charitable beneficiary designation
2. Dissolution or merger of the Company
3. Transfer of substantially all assets
4. Amendment to the Operating Agreement's core provisions

---

### ARTICLE VII: PURPOSE AND POWERS

**Section 7.1 - Purpose**

The purpose of the Company is to:

(a) Develop, maintain, and govern the SynSync Pro brainwave entrainment platform and related technologies;
(b) Manage decentralized finance protocols, including the Timebank mutual aid system and Sheckle cryptocurrency;
(c) Allocate resources toward public benefit objectives, including the mandatory charitable commitment set forth herein;
(d) Enter into licensing agreements, partnerships, and commercial arrangements consistent with the DAO's mission;
(e) Engage in any lawful act or activity for which limited liability companies may be formed under Wyoming law.

**Section 7.2 - Charitable Commitment**

The Company hereby irrevocably commits to the following charitable obligations:

(a) **Mandatory Allocation:** Ten percent (10%) of all net protocol revenue, token sale proceeds, and treasury inflows shall be allocated to the Charitable Beneficiary specified in Article VIII.

(b) **Distribution Mechanism:** Charitable distributions shall be made quarterly through:
   - Direct cryptocurrency transfers to the beneficiary wallet
   - Conversion to fiat through approved service providers
   - Smart contract-automated distributions where technically feasible

(c) **Binding Nature:** This charitable commitment is binding on the Company, its Members, and any successor entities. Amendment or termination requires:
   - Supermajority vote of 80% of total voting power
   - Replacement beneficiary designation meeting the criteria in Article VIII
   - Compliance with Wyoming law regarding material modifications to LLC purpose

**Section 7.3 - Public Benefit Purpose**

The Company is organized to operate for the public benefit by:
1. Developing accessible wellness technology for underserved populations
2. Creating economic opportunity through decentralized finance
3. Supporting anti-trafficking initiatives through charitable giving
4. Promoting open-source software and privacy-preserving technology

No change to the charitable beneficiary shall be effective unless approved by:
- Eighty percent (80%) of total voting power participating in the vote
- A quorum of at least 40% of total voting power
- A 21-day notice period with beneficiary impact disclosure
