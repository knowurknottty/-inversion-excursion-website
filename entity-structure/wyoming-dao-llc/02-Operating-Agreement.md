## ARTICLE 1: FORMATION AND PURPOSE

### 1.1 Formation

This Operating Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of the Effective Date by and among the Members of SYNCHRONICITY DAO LLC, a Wyoming Decentralized Autonomous Organization Limited Liability Company (the "Company" or the "DAO"). The Company was formed by the filing of Articles of Organization with the Wyoming Secretary of State.

### 1.2 Purpose

The Company is organized to:

(a) **Primary Mission:** Develop, govern, and maintain decentralized technology protocols that advance human wellbeing, economic sovereignty, and privacy-preserving innovation;

(b) **Technology Development:** Build and maintain the SynSync Pro brainwave entrainment platform, Timebank mutual aid protocol, Sheckle cryptocurrency, and related open-source technologies;

(c) **Charitable Mandate:** Fulfill the binding charitable commitment to allocate ten percent (10%) of net revenues to Polaris Project or successor qualified charitable organizations;

(d) **IP Commercialization:** License intellectual property to and from affiliated entities in accordance with this Agreement;

(e) **Economic Innovation:** Demonstrate sustainable decentralized business models that align profit generation with public benefit.

### 1.3 Public Benefit Statement

The Company acknowledges its status as a public benefit-oriented organization. While not a nonprofit entity, the Company commits to:
- Prioritizing long-term stakeholder value over short-term extraction
- Maintaining open-source licensing for core protocols
- Ensuring accessibility of wellness technology to underserved populations
- Operating with transparency and accountability to token holders and the public

## ARTICLE 2: DEFINITIONS

### 2.1 Defined Terms

**"Blockchain"** means the distributed ledger technology used by the Company for governance and record-keeping.

**"Charitable Beneficiary"** means Polaris Project or any successor qualified 501(c)(3) organization designated in accordance with Article 6.

**"Charitable Commitment"** means the binding obligation to allocate ten percent (10%) of Net Revenue to the Charitable Beneficiary.

**"DAO Token"** means the governance token issued by the Company representing membership interests and voting rights.

**"Foundation Entity"** means the nonprofit entity established in the Marshall Islands or Switzerland to receive charitable distributions.

**"IP Holding Entity"** means the intellectual property holding company established in the Cayman Islands or British Virgin Islands.

**"Member"** means any person or entity holding DAO Tokens, subject to the requirements of Section 3.1.

**"Net Revenue"** means all gross receipts less refunds, returns, chargebacks, and payment processing fees.

**"Protocol Revenue"** means all fees, premiums, or other value captured by Company smart contracts.

**"Qualified Majority"** means approval by at least sixty-six percent (66%) of voting power participating in a vote.

**"Service Provider"** means any individual or entity engaged by the DAO to perform operational services.

**"Smart Contract"** means self-executing code deployed on a blockchain that automates governance or economic functions.

**"Stewardship Council"** means the body designated in Article 8 to handle legal compliance and emergency matters.

**"Supermajority"** means approval by at least eighty percent (80%) of voting power participating in a vote.

**"Treasury"** means the collective assets held in Company-controlled wallets and accounts.

## ARTICLE 3: MEMBERSHIP AND CAPITAL ACCOUNTS

### 3.1 Eligibility for Membership

Membership in the Company is obtained by:
(a) Holding DAO Tokens in a wallet registered with the Company; and
(b) Agreeing to be bound by this Operating Agreement; and
(c) Not being a Prohibited Person under Section 3.2.

### 3.2 Prohibited Persons

The following may not be Members:
(a) Persons on any sanctions list maintained by OFAC, UN, EU, or comparable authority;
(b) Persons located in jurisdictions where DAO participation is prohibited by law;
(c) Persons who have been convicted of fraud, embezzlement, or financial crimes;
(d) Any entity majority-owned by the foregoing.

### 3.3 Capital Accounts

Each Member shall have a capital account maintained on the blockchain ledger reflecting:
(a) Initial token holdings and acquisition cost;
(b) Share of profits and losses allocated under Article 7;
(c) Distributions received;
(d) Token transfers and sales.

### 3.4 Transfer of Membership Interests

(a) DAO Tokens are freely transferable on public blockchains, subject to compliance with securities laws;
(b) Transferees automatically become Members upon token acquisition;
(c) Transfer restrictions, if any, must be encoded in the token smart contract;
(d) No Member approval is required for token transfers.

### 3.5 Withdrawal and Expulsion

(a) **Voluntary Withdrawal:** A Member may exit by selling or transferring all DAO Tokens;
(b) **Expulsion:** The DAO may expel a Member by Supermajority vote for:
   - Material breach of this Agreement;
   - Engagement in prohibited activities;
   - Actions causing material harm to the DAO;
(c) **Effect of Expulsion:** Expelled Members forfeit governance rights but retain economic rights to distributions accrued prior to expulsion.

## ARTICLE 4: GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

### 4.1 Decentralized Governance Principles

The Company is governed according to these principles:
(a) **Transparency:** All governance proposals, votes, and treasury transactions are publicly visible on the blockchain;
(b) **Participation:** Any Member may propose actions and vote proportionally to token holdings;
(c) **Immutability:** Governance decisions are recorded immutably on the blockchain;
(d) **Upgradeability:** Governance mechanisms may be improved through the amendment process.

### 4.2 Voting Power

(a) Each DAO Token confers one (1) vote on governance matters;
(b) Voting power may be delegated to other Members;
(c) Quadratic voting mechanisms may be implemented to prevent plutocracy;
(d) Voting power snapshots are taken at proposal submission for anti-manipulation.

### 4.3 Proposal Types and Requirements

| Proposal Type | Threshold | Notice Period | Execution |
|--------------|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| Routine Operations | Simple Majority (50%+1) | 3 days | Automatic |
| Treasury Expenditure >$100K | Qualified Majority (66%) | 7 days | Timelock 48hrs |
| Smart Contract Upgrade | Qualified Majority (66%) | 14 days | Timelock 72hrs |
| Operating Agreement Amendment | Supermajority (80%) | 21 days | Timelock 7 days |
| **Charitable Beneficiary Change** | **Supermajority (80%)** | **21 days** | **Timelock 14 days** |
| Dissolution | Supermajority (80%) | 30 days | Timelock 30 days |

### 4.4 Voting Process

(a) **Proposal Submission:** Any Member may submit a proposal by staking [X] DAO Tokens;
(b) **Discussion Period:** Minimum [3/7/14/21/30] days based on proposal type;
(c) **Voting Period:** [3/5/7] days after discussion period;
(d) **Execution:** Passed proposals execute automatically via smart contract or through designated executors;
(e) **Veto Authority:** The Stewardship Council may delay execution of proposals that violate law or this Agreement, subject to DAO override.

### 4.5 Emergency Powers

In circumstances posing imminent threat to the DAO or legal jeopardy:
(a) The Stewardship Council may take emergency actions;
(b) Emergency actions must be ratified by the DAO within 14 days;
(c) Unauthorized emergency actions may result in personal liability for Council members.

## ARTICLE 6: CHARITABLE COMMITMENTS AND BENEFICIARY PROTECTION

### 6.1 Binding Charitable Commitment

**THIS SECTION CONSTITUTES A MATERIAL TERM OF THE COMPANY'S ORGANIZATIONAL DOCUMENTS AND MAY NOT BE AMENDED WITHOUT SUPERMAJORITY APPROVAL.**

### 6.2 Mandatory Allocation

The Company irrevocably commits to allocate **TEN PERCENT (10%)** of all Net Revenue to the Charitable Beneficiary.

### 6.3 Revenue Categories Subject to Allocation

The 10% charitable allocation applies to:
(a) Protocol fees and usage charges;
(b) Token sale proceeds (primary sales);
(c) Licensing revenue from IP Holding Entity;
(d) Investment returns on Treasury assets;
(e) Any other income derived from DAO operations.

### 6.4 Calculation and Timing

(a) **Calculation:** Allocations calculated quarterly based on Net Revenue;
(b) **Timing:** Distributions made within 30 days of quarter end;
(c) **Form:** May be distributed in cryptocurrency or converted to fiat;
(d) **Documentation:** All charitable transfers recorded on blockchain and in financial statements.

### 6.5 Named Beneficiary

**Initial Charitable Beneficiary:**

**Polaris Project**
- Legal Name: Polaris
- EIN: 54-1647272
- Address: P.O. Box 7785, Washington, DC 20044
- Website: https://polarisproject.org/
- Mission: Eradicating sex trafficking and supporting survivors
- Designated Wallet Address: [ETH/SOL Address]

### 6.6 SUPERMAJORITY PROTECTION - BENEFICIARY CHANGES

**NO CHANGE TO THE CHARITABLE BENEFICIARY SHALL BE VALID UNLESS:**

(a) **Voting Threshold:** Approved by Supermajority (80%) of voting power actually participating in the vote;

(b) **Quorum Requirement:** At least 40% of total outstanding voting power participates;

(c) **Notice Period:** Minimum 21 days advance notice including:
   - Detailed rationale for proposed change;
   - Analysis of impact on charitable mission;
   - Due diligence on proposed new beneficiary;
   - Comparison of proposed vs. current beneficiary;

(d) **Replacement Criteria:** Any new beneficiary must satisfy:
   - 501(c)(3) public charity in good standing;
   - Mission alignment with human rights, anti-trafficking, or technology accessibility;
   - Minimum five (5) years operational history;
   - Annual expenditures exceeding $1,000,000;
   - GuideStar/Candid Platinum or Gold rating;
   - Demonstrated financial transparency;

(e) **Timelock:** Approved changes take effect no sooner than 14 days after vote completion;

(f) **Escalation:** Changes to beneficiaries with less than 5-year history require 90% approval.

### 6.7 Reduction or Suspension of Charitable Allocation

(a) **NO REDUCTION:** The 10% charitable allocation may not be reduced for any reason;

(b) **Temporary Suspension:** In extraordinary circumstances (prolonged insolvency, regulatory prohibition), the DAO may suspend charitable distributions by Supermajority vote for up to 180 days;

(c) **Catch-Up Obligation:** Any suspended allocations must be paid in full once circumstances permit;

(d) **Permanent Elimination:** PERMANENT ELIMINATION OF THE CHARITABLE COMMITMENT IS PROHIBITED.

### 6.8 Beneficiary Rights

(a) The Charitable Beneficiary is an express third-party beneficiary of this Agreement;
(b) The Beneficiary may enforce charitable allocation rights through legal action;
(c) The Beneficiary shall receive quarterly reports on allocation calculations;
(d) The Beneficiary may participate in governance discussions but holds no voting rights.

### 6.9 Foundation Entity as Intermediary

(a) The DAO may distribute charitable allocations through the Foundation Entity;
(b) The Foundation Entity shall be bound to forward all received funds to the ultimate Charitable Beneficiary;
(c) Foundation Entity operating expenses may not exceed 5% of distributions;
(d) The DAO may revoke the Foundation Entity's intermediary status by Qualified Majority vote.

## ARTICLE 10: TAX AND REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

### 10.1 Tax Classification

(a) The Company shall file IRS Form 8832 to elect partnership taxation (default) or corporate taxation as determined by tax counsel;
(b) Members shall receive annual K-1s (if partnership) or 1099s as applicable;
(c) The DAO shall maintain records sufficient for Member tax reporting.

### 10.2 Regulatory Compliance

(a) The Company shall comply with applicable securities, tax, and AML laws;
(b) Token distributions may be subject to vesting or transfer restrictions to comply with securities law;
(c) The DAO shall implement travel rule compliance for large transfers.

### 10.3 Charitable Tax Treatment

(a) Charitable allocations are not deductible by the Company (pass-through entity);
(b) Members may deduct their share of charitable allocations subject to IRS limitations;
(c) The Company shall provide Members with documentation for charitable deductions.

## ARTICLE 11: DISSOLUTION AND WINDING UP

### 11.1 Dissolution Events

The Company shall dissolve upon:
(a) Supermajority vote for dissolution;
(b) Court order;
(c) Failure to maintain registered agent for 60+ days;
(d) Bankruptcy or insolvency.

### 11.2 Winding Up Process

Upon dissolution:
(a) Stewardship Council appointed as liquidators;
(b) Assets liquidated and debts paid;
(c) Charitable allocations paid to current beneficiary;
(d) Remaining assets distributed to Members.

### 11.3 Asset Distribution Priority

1. Creditors and liabilities;
2. Accrued charitable allocations;
3. Member capital contributions;
4. Remaining assets pro rata to Members.

### 11.4 IP Asset Treatment

Upon dissolution:
(a) IP assets revert to IP Holding Entity;
(b) Core protocols open-sourced under permissive license;
(c) No IP assets distributed to individual Members without compensation to Treasury.
