## ARTICLES OF ASSOCIATION

### PRELIMINARY

**1. Definitions**

"Act" means the Companies Act (As Revised) of the Cayman Islands.
"Board" means the board of directors of the Company.
"DAO" means Synchronicity DAO LLC, a Wyoming limited liability company.
"Director" means a director of the Company.
"IP Assets" means all intellectual property rights held by the Company.
"Licensing Agreement" means the Master IP Licensing Agreement between the Company and the DAO.
"Member" means a holder of shares in the Company.
"Officer" means any officer appointed by the Board.

### SHARES AND SHAREHOLDERS

**2. Share Capital**

(a) The authorized share capital is US$50,000 divided into 50,000 ordinary shares of US$1.00 each.
(b) The Board may issue shares with such rights and restrictions as they determine.
(c) All shares shall be fully paid upon issuance.

**3. Share Transfers**

(a) Shares are transferable subject to Board approval.
(b) No transfer may result in the DAO holding less than 51% of issued shares without DAO consent.
(c) The Board may refuse to register a transfer if it would violate any shareholders' agreement.

**4. Shareholder Register**

The Company shall maintain a register of members at its registered office.

### DIRECTORS AND MANAGEMENT

**5. Board Composition**

(a) The Board shall consist of not less than one (1) and not more than seven (7) directors.
(b) Initial Directors appointed by the Subscriber:
   - [Director Name 1]
   - [Director Name 2]
   - [Director Name 3]

**6. Director Qualifications**

(a) Directors need not be shareholders.
(b) At least one Director shall be a nominee of the DAO.
(c) Directors shall have experience in intellectual property law, technology, or corporate governance.

**7. Powers of Directors**

The Board shall manage the business and affairs of the Company, including:
(a) Entering into the Licensing Agreement with the DAO;
(b) Prosecuting and maintaining IP registrations;
(c) Enforcing IP rights against infringers;
(d) Approving sublicenses to third parties;
(e) Managing Company assets and investments;
(f) Fulfilling obligations under the Licensing Agreement.

**8. Director Limitations**

The Board may NOT without DAO approval:
(a) Sell, assign, or encumber material IP Assets;
(b) Amend the Licensing Agreement to reduce DAO rights;
(c) Declare dividends in excess of available distributable reserves;
(d) Merge, consolidate, or dissolve the Company;
(e) Change the Company's name or jurisdiction of incorporation.

**9. Meetings of Directors**

(a) Meetings may be held in person, by telephone, or by video conference.
(b) A quorum consists of a majority of Directors.
(c) Resolutions may be passed by written consent signed by all Directors.
(d) The DAO nominee Director may call emergency meetings.

**10. Conflicts of Interest**

(a) Directors shall disclose any conflicts of interest.
(b) A Director interested in a transaction may not vote on it.
(c) The Licensing Agreement with the DAO is approved, and DAO-affiliated Directors may participate in related votes.

### PROTECTION OF IP ASSETS

**11. IP Asset Protection Mandate**

(a) The Company shall maintain all IP Assets in trust for the benefit of the DAO and its mission.
(b) IP Assets shall not be used to secure debt without DAO approval.
(c) The Company shall maintain appropriate insurance for IP enforcement and defense.
(d) The Company shall implement cybersecurity measures to protect digital IP assets.

**12. Licensing Revenue**

(a) Revenue from the Licensing Agreement shall be applied as specified therein.
(b) Net revenue after expenses may be distributed as dividends to shareholders or reinvested.
(c) Priority shall be given to maintaining and expanding the IP portfolio.

### DIVIDENDS AND RESERVES

**13. Dividends**

(a) The Board may declare dividends from distributable reserves.
(b) Dividend declarations require approval of shareholders holding 75% of shares.
(c) The DAO shall receive preferential dividend rights on its shareholding.

**14. Reserves**

The Board may establish reserves for:
(a) IP litigation and enforcement;
(b) Patent maintenance fees and renewals;
(c) Insurance premiums;
(d) General corporate purposes.

### ACCOUNTS AND AUDIT

**15. Financial Records**

(a) The Company shall maintain proper books of account.
(b) Accounts shall be prepared annually in accordance with international accounting standards.
(c) Accounts shall be audited by independent auditors appointed by the Board.

**16. Audit Rights**

(a) The DAO shall have the right to appoint observers to review audit processes.
(b) Audited financial statements shall be provided to the DAO within 90 days of year-end.

### INDEMNIFICATION

**17. Indemnification**

(a) The Company shall indemnify Directors and Officers to the fullest extent permitted by law.
(b) Indemnification excludes willful misconduct, fraud, or knowing violations of law.
(c) The Company may purchase D&O insurance.

### DISSOLUTION

**18. Winding Up**

Upon winding up:
(a) IP Assets shall be transferred to the DAO or its designee.
(b) Remaining assets distributed to shareholders pro rata.
(c) The DAO has a right of first refusal on IP Asset transfers.

### AMENDMENTS

**19. Amendment of Articles**

These Articles may be amended by special resolution (75% majority) of shareholders, provided that no amendment may:
(a) Materially reduce DAO rights under the Licensing Agreement without DAO consent;
(b) Eliminate the DAO's right to appoint a Director nominee;
(c) Change the Company's jurisdiction of incorporation without 90% shareholder approval.

(c) No new shares may be issued that would dilute the DAO below 51% without DAO consent.

### ARTICLE 3: GOVERNANCE AND BOARD COMPOSITION

**3.1 Board Composition**

The Board shall consist of five (5) directors:
- Three (3) appointed by the DAO;
- One (1) appointed by Strategic Investor;
- One (1) independent director agreed by all Shareholders.

**3.2 Reserved Matters**

The following require approval of 75% of directors OR DAO written consent:
(a) Sale or license of material IP Assets;
(b) Amendment or termination of the Licensing Agreement;
(c) Incurring debt exceeding US$100,000;
(d) Related party transactions (excluding the Licensing Agreement);
(e) Change in auditors or registered agent;
(f) Amendment to constitutional documents.

**3.3 DAO Veto Rights**

The DAO may veto:
(a) Any sale of core IP Assets;
(b) Any change to the Licensing Agreement that reduces DAO rights;
(c) Any action that would impair the charitable mission of the DAO.

### ARTICLE 4: LICENSING AGREEMENT OBLIGATIONS

**4.1 Licensing Agreement Execution**

The Company shall execute and maintain the Master IP Licensing Agreement with the DAO on terms no less favorable than those set forth in Exhibit A.

**4.2 License Terms**

(a) **Grant:** Exclusive, perpetual, worldwide license to use IP Assets for DAO's protocols.
(b) **Sublicensing:** DAO may sublicense to open-source communities.
(c) **Consideration:** Annual license fee of US$[X] or [X]% of DAO protocol revenue, whichever is greater.
(d) **Improvements:** IP improvements developed by DAO remain DAO property but licensed back to Company.

**4.3 Most Favored Licensee**

If the Company grants more favorable terms to any third party, such terms shall automatically apply to the DAO.

### ARTICLE 5: TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS

**5.1 General Restrictions**

No Shareholder may transfer shares without:
(a) Offering shares first to the Company (right of redemption);
(b) Then offering to other Shareholders pro rata (right of first refusal);
(c) Obtaining Board approval of transferee.

**5.2 DAO Transfer Rights**

The DAO may freely transfer shares to:
(a) Its treasury or governance contracts;
(b) Affiliated entities controlled by the DAO;
(c) For charitable or public benefit purposes.

**5.3 Tag-Along Rights**

If a majority shareholder sells to a third party, minority shareholders may participate on the same terms.

**5.4 Drag-Along Rights**

If holders of 75% of shares approve a sale of the Company, all shareholders must participate.

### ARTICLE 6: FINANCIAL MATTERS

**6.1 Dividend Policy**

(a) Minimum 50% of annual net profits distributed as dividends.
(b) Remaining profits reinvested in IP portfolio expansion.
(c) Special dividends require 75% shareholder approval.

**6.2 Budget Approval**

Annual budgets require Board approval with DAO consent for material variances.

### ARTICLE 7: INFORMATION RIGHTS

**7.1 DAO Information Rights**

The DAO shall receive:
(a) Monthly management reports;
(b) Quarterly financial statements;
(c) Annual audited financial statements;
(d) Immediate notice of material events;
(e) Access to all IP registration and maintenance records.

### ARTICLE 8: NON-COMPETE AND CONFIDENTIALITY

**8.1 Non-Compete**

Shareholders may not directly compete with the Company's IP licensing business.

**8.2 Confidentiality**

All parties maintain confidentiality of proprietary information.
