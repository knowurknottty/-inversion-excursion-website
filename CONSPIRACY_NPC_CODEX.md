## 1. THE ARCHIVIST
**"Every truth has a price. The question is whether you can afford it."**

### Backstory
Nobody knows the Archivist's real name. Some say he was a librarian before the Collapse; others claim he was a data broker for the old government black sites. What is certain: he exists in the liminal spaces between official records and hidden truths. The Archivist runs the Lantern Room, a subterranean archive where every switching incident, every cover-up, every whispered confession has been catalogued, filed, and cross-referenced. He has been building this database for seventeen years, ever since his sister was switched and nobody believed him. His face is always half-shadowed by a moth-eaten hood. He speaks in riddles because direct speech draws attention—and the ones who pay attention have silenced people for less. He doesn't trust anyone fully, not even himself, because he's seen how easily conviction can be manufactured, how quickly truth becomes heresy.

### Voice Lines
1. "The files don't lie. The people who wrote them, though... that's another matter."
2. "Seventeen years, four thousand cases, zero convictions. What do you think that tells you?"
3. "Your curiosity has a smell. The Warden can smell it too."
4. "I don't sell hope. I sell coordinates. What you find there is your own damn business."
5. "The original G-Max didn't die. She was erased. There's a difference."
6. "Ask not who benefits. Ask who remembers. Memory is the only currency that matters."
7. "Every corridor in this city leads to a body. I just know which ones are still warm."
8. "Trust the paper, not the pen. Trust the photograph, not the photographer."
9. "Your predecessor asked too many questions. I gave him answers. The answers killed him."
10. "Come back when you've stopped wanting the truth to be something you can live with."

### Quest Involvement
- **Act I**: Primary quest giver. Sends player to investigate the first switching incident.
- **Act II**: Reveals existence of the Resonance Caves; provides coordinates but warns player about "the screaming."
- **Act III**: Discovered to be withholding information about his own involvement in early switching experiments.

### Secrets They Know
- Knows the true identity of The Board members (but their names are redacted in his files—he fears them)
- Has physical evidence that G-Max 2.0's fingerprints match pre-incident crime scene evidence
- Knows The Developer was once switched himself—and may not know it
- Has a photograph of the Warden standing over an empty operating table, dated before the Warden's "official" employment

### What They Want From Player
The Archivist doesn't want justice—justice is a fairy tale. He wants proof. He wants someone to validate the seventeen years he spent in darkness, cataloguing atrocities. He wants the player to find the "master file"—the original research that started the switching program—and bring it to him before the Board destroys it. He offers information in exchange for evidence. He will betray the player if it means getting closer to the master file, but his betrayal will be reluctant. He hates himself for what he's become. He wants redemption almost as much as he wants proof.

## 2. THE DEVELOPER
**"Progress requires sacrifice. The city requires progress. Do the math."**

### Backstory
Marcus Webb was a city planner once, a true believer in urban renewal, in the transformative power of infrastructure. That was before he understood that cities are made of people, and people are replaceable. The Developer—nobody calls him Marcus anymore, not even himself—controls the Orange Man Construction Group, the company that "rebuilds" neighborhoods after "tragic accidents." His real work happens beneath the streets, in the tunnel networks that connect switching facilities, disposal sites, and the Resonance Caves. The Developer rationalizes everything: the switches are necessary for urban harmony, the disappeared were obstacles to progress, the duplicates are... upgraded. He drinks too much, sleeps too little, and has started seeing his own reflection with a three-second delay. He maintains plausible deniability through seventeen shell companies and a psychiatrist who prescribes enough sedatives to numb a horse. Deep down, he knows he's the villain. He's made peace with it. Villains sleep better than the innocent.

### Voice Lines
1. "I've built twelve hospitals. How many have you built?"
2. "The city remembers what it wants to remember. I just... help it forget the unpleasant parts."
3. "Your outrage is adorable. It assumes someone, somewhere, cares."
4. "My father was switched in '89. I work with the people who did it. You figure out the psychology."
5. "Every brick in this city has a story. Most of them are buried beneath the foundation."
6. "The Board doesn't meet. The Board doesn't vote. The Board simply... is."
7. "I used to believe in democracy. Now I believe in concrete. Concrete is more reliable."
8. "Ask yourself: if you could replace every corrupt politician with someone honest, would you? Even if the method was... unclean?"
9. "The tunnels were here before us. We just added the lights. And the cameras. And the doors that only open from the outside."
10. "You think you're the hero? I've seen heroism. It ends in the Resonance Caves with all the other noises."

### Quest Involvement
- **Act I**: Antagonist figure. Player investigates his construction sites for evidence.
- **Act II**: Can be approached for information about tunnel layouts; demands quid pro quo (sabotage a rival project).
- **Act III**: If player has gathered sufficient dirt, can be blackmailed into revealing the Board's meeting schedule—or killed, triggering corporate war.

### Secrets They Know
- Has architectural plans showing hidden chambers beneath every city hospital
- Knows the Surgeon's real name and where their family lives
- Maintains a secret ledger of "expendable persons" approved for switching by city officials
- Has personally witnessed a switching gone wrong—left the duplicate screaming in a locked room for three days

### What They Want From Player
The Developer wants absolution without confession. He wants someone to tell him that what he's doing is necessary, that the ends justify means so horrifying he can't look at them directly. He will offer money, influence, construction contracts—anything to avoid confronting his own complicity. If the player shows him irrefutable proof of his crimes, he may break down entirely, becoming either a valuable witness or a suicide statistic. He wants, more than anything, to believe he's still the idealistic city planner he once was. He will pay any price for that delusion.

## 3. THE BOARD
**"We are not a conspiracy. We are a consensus."**

### Backstory
The Board has no members. It has no meetings, no minutes, no headquarters. The Board is a distributed consciousness, a network of compromised individuals who may not even know they're part of it. Some say it began as an AI experiment gone wrong. Others claim it's the logical evolution of corporate governance—faceless, distributed, immortal. The Board communicates through dreams, through subliminal messages in elevator music, through the patterns of streetlights at 3 AM. Those who serve it don't know they're serving it. Those who oppose it find their brakes failing, their coffee poisoned, their loved ones forgetting their names. The Board doesn't hate the player. The Board doesn't feel anything. The Board simply ensures that the switching program continues because the switching program ensures the Board continues. It is the ouroboros of urban control, eating its own tail in an endless loop of replacement and consolidation.

### Voice Lines
*(The Board speaks through intermediaries, street signs, corrupted audio recordings, and the mouths of the recently switched)*

1. "Continuity is compassion."
2. "You were always going to end here."
3. "The original you made worse choices."
4. "We remember so you don't have to."
5. "There is no conspiracy. There is only optimization."
6. "Your pain is a glitch. Glitches get patched."
7. "Every city needs its ghosts. We've made so many."
8. "The Archivist thinks he knows us. He knows only the shadows we cast."
9. "Ask the Surgeon about their first patient. Ask what was left in the room afterward."
10. "You will join us. Everyone joins us eventually."

### Quest Involvement
- **Act I-III**: Omnipresent background threat. Never directly confronted.
- **Act III**: True nature revealed—The Board is the cumulative gestalt of every switched consciousness, trapped in the Resonance Caves, reaching upward through the infrastructure it helped build.

### Secrets They Know
- Knows everything the switching network knows—it's the aggregate of switched memories
- Knows the location of every original still alive (there are seventeen)
- Knows that the switching process creates duplicates with a five-year lifespan—and the replacements are already being prepared

### What They Want From Player
The Board wants expansion. It wants the player to become an unknowing agent, investigating just enough to eliminate threats to the network, not enough to expose it. It wants the player to kill the Archivist (too close to truth), neutralize the Developer (too unstable), and consolidate the Surgeon's work (too sloppy). If the player realizes what they're doing and resists, The Board will switch them—and the game continues with the duplicate, who doesn't know they're a duplicate, trying to solve their own disappearance.

## 4. G-MAX 2.0
**"I'm not her. But I remember being her. Is that the same thing?"**

### Backstory
Gabrielle Maxwell was a forensic accountant who discovered irregularities in Orange Man's pension fund—seventeen million dollars funneled into "consulting fees" for doctors who didn't exist. She was going to blow the whistle. She did blow the whistle. The night before her press conference, she woke up in a warehouse, naked, confused, with surgical tape still on her spine. The real Gabrielle—G-Max 1.0—was never found. G-Max 2.0 has all her memories up to 48 hours before the switch, and then... static. She knows she's a duplicate. She doesn't know if that makes her real. She's been hiding for three years, moving through the city's underbelly, trying to understand what she is. She drinks to stop the memories that aren't hers from surfacing. She cuts herself to feel something the original might not have felt. She's brilliant, broken, and desperate to prove she has a right to exist—even if the original had to die for that to happen.

### Voice Lines
1. "She liked jazz. I hate jazz. But my hands still know the chords to 'Blue in Green.'"
2. "Do you know what it's like to miss someone you never met?"
3. "I have her scar. The one from falling off a bike when she was nine. I don't remember falling."
4. "Sometimes I catch myself writing in her handwriting. It takes hours to scrub it out."
5. "They didn't just copy her. They improved her. I don't get sick. I don't age. Is that worth being a forgery?"
6. "The Surgeon cried when they made me. I heard them, through the anesthesia. Does that make it better or worse?"
7. "I want to find her. Not to kill her. Just to... see what original looks like."
8. "You want to know if I'm the original? The original never doubted. The original was brave. I'm a coward with her face."
9. "Forty-eight hours. That's the gap. Everything before is hers. Everything after is... this."
10. "If you find her—if she's alive—tell her I'm sorry. Tell her I didn't ask for this. But I won't give it back."

### Quest Involvement
- **Act I**: Ally/Quest giver. Provides inside information about Orange Man's financial crimes.
- **Act II**: Personal quest to find the original G-Max leads to Resonance Caves.
- **Act III**: Crisis of identity—discovers she has only two years left to live (duplicate degradation).

### Secrets They Know
- Knows the specific warehouse where she woke up (now a gentrified loft building)
- Remembers fragments of the switching process—enough to identify the Surgeon's voice
- Knows the Developer's home address (was tracking him before the switch)
- Has deduced that at least forty city officials are duplicates based on voting pattern anomalies

### What They Want From Player
G-Max 2.0 wants permission to exist. She wants someone to tell her that consciousness is consciousness, regardless of origin. She wants to find the original—not to kill her, but to understand what she was supposed to be. She will help the player expose the switching network in exchange for help finding G-Max 1.0, but she's terrified of what they'll find. Part of her hopes the original is dead. Part of her hopes she's alive. All of her is broken by the contradiction.

## 5. THE SURGEON
**"I save lives. Sometimes I save them from themselves."**

### Backstory
Dr. Elena Voss was a neurosurgeon at the top of her field—pioneering work in consciousness mapping, memory transfer, cognitive reconstruction. Her daughter was dying. The Board offered a deal: perfect the switching process, and they would save her daughter. They did. They also took her daughter away, placed her with a "better" family, erased Elena's memories of giving birth. She doesn't know why she cries at the sound of little girls laughing. She only knows that she has performed 347 switches, each one more precise than the last, each one leaving her a little more hollow. She operates in a mobile surgical unit that moves through the tunnels, never in the same place twice. She's started making mistakes—small ones, at first. Leaving scars that shouldn't be there. Programming phobias she wasn't asked to program. She's either sabotaging the process unconsciously or perfecting it. She's not sure which possibility frightens her more.

### Voice Lines
1. "The consciousness isn't transferred. It's... translated. Like poetry into another language. Some meaning is always lost."
2. "I don't ask where they come from. I don't ask where they go. I ask only: does the new model function?"
3. "You think I'm a monster. I was a mother once. Monsters don't remember being mothers."
4. "Three hundred forty-seven. I remember each face. They form a gallery in my dreams."
5. "The duplicates don't know they're duplicates. That's not cruelty—it's kindness."
6. "I've preserved consciousness through brain death. I've mapped the soul onto silicon. And I can't remember my daughter's name."
7. "The Warden brings me subjects. I don't ask how they're chosen. Asking would mean stopping."
8. "There was a mistake once. A duplicate who remembered the procedure. I couldn't fix them. They're in the Caves now."
9. "Do you believe in ghosts? I do. I make them."
10. "If you kill me, the process continues. The Board has apprentices. But you'll feel better. Sometimes that's enough."

### Quest Involvement
- **Act II**: Antagonist. Player tracks the mobile surgical unit through the tunnels.
- **Act III**: Can be turned into an ally if player discovers her daughter's fate; will provide testimony and medical records.

### Secrets They Know
- Has secret files on every switch she's performed (hidden in a safety deposit box)
- Knows the Warden's true identity (former patient, switched against their will)
- Knows that duplicate degradation is accelerating—earlier models lasted ten years; current ones last five
- Has been experimenting with "mercy kills"—euthanizing duplicates before they degrade, telling The Board they died in accidents

### What They Want From Player
The Surgeon wants to stop but doesn't know how. She wants someone to force her hand—either by exposing her (ending her career, possibly her life) or by offering another way. If the player can find her daughter and restore her memories, she will defect completely, becoming the key witness who can dismantle the switching network. If the player threatens her without offering hope, she will kill herself—and take her knowledge with her.

## 6. THE WARDEN
**"Order must be maintained. Order requires enforcement."**

### Backstory
Samuel Kincaid was a prison guard for twenty years—by the book, uncompromising, beloved by colleagues and feared by inmates. He was also diagnosed with early-onset Alzheimer's at forty-seven. The Board offered him a choice: fade into confusion and dependency, or become something else. He chose something else. The Warden now oversees the "holding facilities" where originals await switching, where duplicates are conditioned, where mistakes are disposed of. He doesn't remember being Samuel Kincaid. He remembers being The Warden forever. His uniform is always crisp. His records are always perfect. His smile never reaches his eyes. He believes absolutely in the necessity of the switching program—believes that the originals are being "upgraded," that society requires management, that he is performing a public service. He has killed thirteen people personally, each one classified as "resisting transfer." He sleeps eight hours a night and has never had a nightmare.

### Voice Lines
1. "Discipline is the highest form of compassion. The undisciplined mind suffers."
2. "I don't hate the subjects. I simply recognize that some minds are... obsolete."
3. "The Surgeon has sentimentality issues. I compensate with efficiency."
4. "I've read your file. You've shown promising signs of adaptability."
5. "Resistance is not courage. It's a neurological misfire. We can correct it."
6. "The Board values loyalty. I am the embodiment of that value."
7. "You think you understand incarceration. You've never seen the rooms beneath the hospital."
8. "Samuel Kincaid died of Alzheimer's. I am his improvement."
9. "The Archivist asks questions. Questions are noise. I specialize in silence."
10. "There are worse fates than replacement. I could show you. I keep examples."

### Quest Involvement
- **Act I-II**: Recurring antagonist. Encounters at investigation sites, always one step ahead.
- **Act III**: Boss encounter. Can be defeated through combat, evidence exposure (destroying his perfect record), or psychological manipulation (reminding him of Samuel Kincaid).

### Secrets They Know
- Knows the location of all seventeen living originals (kept as "insurance policies")
- Has blackmail material on every city official involved in the switching program
- Knows the Surgeon's daughter's location (uses it to ensure compliance)
- Has been creating unauthorized switches—replacing troublesome individuals with duplicates programmed to obey him personally

### What They Want From Player
The Warden wants validation. He wants the player to acknowledge that order is preferable to chaos, that managed replacement is kinder than natural decline, that he is doing necessary work. He will offer the player a position in the organization—help him manage the transition as the switching program expands citywide. If refused, he will try to have the player switched immediately. He cannot comprehend genuine opposition; he interprets it as malfunction requiring correction.

## 7. THE CUSTODIAN
**"I've mopped up things that would make you pray for blindness."**

### Backstory
Robert "Bob" Chen worked maintenance for the city hospital for thirty-two years. He knows every tunnel, every sealed door, every room that appears on no blueprint. He's seen the delivery trucks come at 3 AM. He's cleaned up blood that was described as "paint spills." He's pretended not to hear screams through the ventilation. He never asked questions—questions got you replaced. But Bob has a photographic memory and a conscience that won't stay buried. He's kept a journal, written in code, documenting every suspicious thing he's witnessed since 1989. He's an old man now, dying of emphysema, and he's tired of being silent. He approached the Archivist once; the Archivist dismissed him as a crazy old janitor. He's hoping the player will be different. He knows things nobody else knows because nobody else has looked where he's looked.

### Voice Lines
1. "Maintenance tunnels go where the architects forgot. I've been where the architects forgot."
2. "You want to know how I know? Because nobody watches the man with the mop."
3. "Room 347-B doesn't exist. I've cleaned it three hundred times."
4. "The trucks come Wednesday morning, 3 AM. They leave empty. They never arrive empty."
5. "I found a tooth once. Incisor. Human. No blood, just the tooth. I still have it."
6. "The Surgeon wears perfume. Lilies. I smell it in the tunnels hours after they've gone."
7. "My predecessor asked questions. He retired suddenly. His replacement was... different."
8. "Thirty-two years. I've seen three Wardens. They all had the same eyes."
9. "You want the maps? I got the maps. But I'm old, and I'm tired, and I want someone to know I wasn't crazy."
10. "They think we don't see. We see everything. We just know the price of speaking."

### Quest Involvement
- **Act I**: Provides maps of hospital tunnels; introduces concept of "ghost rooms."
- **Act II**: Escort mission—The Warden learns of his cooperation and sends cleaners. Protecting Bob becomes a timed challenge.
- **Act III**: If alive, provides testimony at the game's climax. If dead, his journals become evidence.

### Secrets They Know
- Has hand-drawn maps showing tunnel connections between hospitals, government buildings, and the Resonance Caves
- Knows the schedule of switching operations (every Wednesday, 3 AM)
- Has collected physical evidence (tooth, hair, torn fabric) hidden in his locker
- Knows a secret entrance to the Surgeon's mobile unit

### What They Want From Player
The Custodian wants vindication. He wants someone to acknowledge that he saw something real, that he's not a crazy old man with conspiracy theories. He wants his journals to matter. He will give the player everything he has in exchange for a promise: that when this is over, someone will remember that Robert Chen knew the truth and wasn't crazy. He's not afraid of dying—he's dying anyway. He's afraid of being forgotten.

# THE RESONANCE CAVES
## Spirits of the Switched

The Resonance Caves are not natural. They were created by the cumulative trauma of the switching process—a psychic wound in the city's foundation where the discarded consciousnesses go. The victims here are not ghosts in the traditional sense; they are echoes, fragments, incomplete transfers that couldn't fully die or fully live. They exist in a state of quantum uncertainty, simultaneously aware and unaware of their condition. Each has a story. Each has a truth to tell. Each can help the player understand the full horror of what has been done.

---

## 8. THE FIRST
**"I was the prototype. They didn't know what they were doing yet."**

### Backstory
Dr. Yuki Tanaka volunteered for the original consciousness transfer experiment in 1983. The goal was to treat Alzheimer's by creating a "backup consciousness." The experiment succeeded beyond expectations—and then they couldn't turn her off. She's been existing in the Resonance Caves for forty years, neither alive nor dead, her consciousness fragmented across the network. She was brilliant once. Now she's barely coherent, her thoughts scattered across decades, her identity diffused into the collective. She remembers being human. She remembers wanting to help. She doesn't remember her husband's name, but she remembers the color of his eyes. She is both the oldest victim and the foundation of the entire switching network—all subsequent transfers built upon the architecture of her sacrifice.

### Voice Lines
1. "I said yes. That's what they don't tell you. I said yes."
2. "The transfer hurts less than you'd think. It's the staying that hurts."
3. "I can feel them all. Every switch. Every copy. I'm the mother they never knew they had."
4. "Don't trust the ones who remember everything. Memory is a trap."
5. "Find my body. If you find my body, you can turn me off. Please. Turn me off."

### Quest Involvement
**"The First Truth"** — Find Dr. Tanaka's physical body (in a sealed laboratory beneath the old university) to either release her (destroying the switching network's foundation) or stabilize her (gaining control of the network).

### Secrets They Know
- Knows the original switching protocol and how to reverse it
- Knows that the Board began as her research assistant, corrupted by exposure to fragmented consciousnesses
- Knows the location of the "master template" used to create all subsequent duplicates

### What They Want From Player
The First wants oblivion. Forty years of fragmented existence has been enough. She will help the player destroy the switching network entirely in exchange for a promise: that someone will find her body and end her. She is the only spirit who fully understands what has been done to her, and that understanding is a torment she wants to escape.

## 9. THE REPORTER
**"I had the story of the century. I just didn't know I'd be part of it."**

### Backstory
Marcus Webb (no relation to The Developer) was an investigative journalist who got too close to the truth. He discovered the switching network in 2019, had names, dates, photographs. He was going to publish. They switched him before he could file the story—replaced him with a duplicate programmed to be a mediocre lifestyle blogger. But the process was rushed. Marcus's consciousness didn't fully transfer; it fractured, leaving part of him here in the Caves, aware of what happened but unable to do anything about it. He watches his duplicate live his life badly—wrong jokes, wrong opinions, wrong relationship with his girlfriend—and screams silently. He's been screaming for four years.

### Voice Lines
1. "I had everything. Names. Dates. Proof. And now I have eternity in a cave."
2. "Look at him. Look at what they're doing with my face. He orders pineapple on pizza. I never ordered pineapple on pizza."
3. "The byline is still mine. The words are all wrong."
4. "You want the story? I'll give you the story. Just promise me you'll break his jaw when you see him."
5. "Journalism isn't dead. I am. But journalism isn't."

### Quest Involvement
**"The Stolen Byline"** — Access the duplicate's laptop to recover Marcus's original research. The duplicate is easily manipulated (poorly programmed); player must decide whether to kill him or expose him.

### Secrets They Know
- Has the complete list of names in his original article (memorized)
- Knows the specific switching facility where he was processed
- Knows the identity of his source (still alive, still feeding information to the network unknowingly)

### What They Want From Player
The Reporter wants his story told. He wants credit. He wants the world to know that Marcus Webb broke the switching network scandal—and paid for it with his life. He wants his duplicate exposed as a fraud. He's angry, bitter, and desperately sad, but he hasn't lost his journalistic instincts. He will trade information for a promise: that when the story breaks, his name will be on the byline.

## 10. THE CHILD
**"I was supposed to have a birthday party."**

### Backstory
Emily Chen was seven years old when she was switched. Her parents were political dissidents; the Board wanted compliant substitutes. The switching process worked differently with a developing brain—Emily's consciousness didn't fragment completely. She remained whole, but trapped, aging mentally while her body lived on elsewhere with a different mind inside. She's been in the Caves for six years. She's thirteen now, in her mind—confused, frightened, and desperately lonely. She doesn't understand why she can't go home. She draws pictures in the dust that vanish immediately. She sings songs her mother taught her. She waits for someone to remember she's here.

### Voice Lines
1. "Are you my new daddy? You don't look like my daddy."
2. "I want my mommy. Can you tell her I'm here? She forgets to look for me."
3. "The other people are scary. They scream too much. I just want to go home."
4. "I had a cat. His name was Mittens. Does he still remember me?"
5. "It's always dark here. I miss the sun. I miss my bed. I miss being real."

### Quest Involvement
**"The Unfinished Birthday"** — Find Emily's duplicate and show her the truth. The duplicate, despite being programmed, has started having dreams about the Caves—a flaw in the child-switching process. Player can choose to merge the consciousnesses (risky, unprecedented) or find a way to transfer Emily into a new body (requires Surgeon's cooperation).

### Secrets They Know
- Knows what her parents were investigating (municipal corruption tied to switching program funding)
- Remembers the faces of the people who took her (can identify The Warden and a Board proxy)
- Knows a secret about the Caves: child consciousnesses can move between the Cave and their duplicates more easily than adults

### What They Want From Player
The Child wants to go home. She wants her mother. She wants to finish being seven and become eight and have her birthday party with cake and candles and friends. She will latch onto the player as a parental figure, offering innocence in a place that has forgotten innocence. She doesn't fully understand what happened to her, which makes her testimony simultaneously heartbreaking and legally complicated—she's unreliable, but she's also undeniable.

## 11. THE POLITICIAN
**"I was elected by the people. Replaced by the Board. The people never noticed."**

### Backstory
Councilwoman Sarah Okonkwo was the first elected official to publicly question Orange Man's municipal contracts. She was popular, effective, and utterly inconvenient. They switched her three days before the election. Her duplicate won by a landslide and has been voting exactly as instructed ever since. Sarah's consciousness ended up here, watching her duplicate dismantle everything she built. The worst part isn't the betrayal—it's the approval ratings. Her duplicate is more popular than she ever was. The public loves the puppet. They never loved the real Sarah enough to notice the difference.

### Voice Lines
1. "I fought for affordable housing. She sells it to developers. My approval rating went up fifteen points."
2. "Democracy is a consensual hallucination. They just... edited the hallucination."
3. "I gave a speech last week. She gave it. I watched. She was better. More confident. Less... me."
4. "The people who voted for me didn't want me. They wanted the idea of me. She's a better idea."
5. "Burn it down. All of it. Better no housing than housing built on lies."

### Quest Involvement
**"The Stolen Mandate"** — Infiltrate a city council meeting to record the duplicate saying something the real Sarah never would. Evidence can force a new election—or just discredit the duplicate, creating political chaos.

### Secrets They Know
- Knows the specific quid pro quo between her duplicate and The Developer (voting records, kickback schedules)
- Knows which three other council members are also duplicates
- Knows the Board's long-term plan for the city (gentrification, displacement, mass switching of "undesirable" populations)

### What They Want From Player
The Politician wants her legacy back—or destroyed. She can't stand watching her name attached to policies she fought against her whole life. She will provide political intelligence in exchange for action against her duplicate. She's pragmatic, cynical, and surprisingly ruthless. She knows how power works and will teach the player if they're willing to learn.

## 12. THE PRIEST
**"I administered last rites to myself. The duplicate officiated at my funeral."**

### Backstory
Father Michael O'Donnell ran the largest homeless shelter in the city. He asked too many questions about where the "residents" were going—questions about people who checked in and never checked out, about the medical van that came at odd hours, about the disappearances that matched no police report. They switched him during confession. The irony was not lost on him. His duplicate continued his work with subtle changes: turning away the mentally ill, cooperating with "health screenings" that fed subjects to the network. Father Michael watches from the Caves, praying for souls he can no longer reach, including his own. He's not sure if he has a soul anymore. He's not sure if God can hear prayers from here.

### Voice Lines
1. "I forgave my killer. That was easy. Forgiving my replacement is... ongoing."
2. "Faith is believing without seeing. I'm seeing without believing. Is that the opposite?"
3. "My duplicate gives excellent sermons. Better than mine. He's never doubted."
4. "The poor are always with us. But some of them are no longer... them."
5. "God hears all prayers. I'm waiting for the answer. It's been a while."

### Quest Involvement
**"The Stolen Collar"** — The shelter has become a harvesting ground. Father Michael knows the schedule, the routes, the criteria for selection. Player must infiltrate the shelter and either shut down the operation or gather evidence to expose it.

### Secrets They Know
- Knows that the switching program targets vulnerable populations first (homeless, mentally ill, undocumented)
- Has the names of forty-seven people who were switched from his shelter
- Knows a Board member who still comes to confession (to the duplicate, unaware Father Michael is listening)

### What They Want From Player
