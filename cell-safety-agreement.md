## SECTION 1: CONFIDENTIALITY

### What Confidentiality Means

I understand that **absolute confidentiality** is a core agreement of this Cell. What is shared within the Cell stays within the Cell. This includes:

- Other members' personal stories and disclosures
- Specific details shared during truth-telling
- Group dynamics and interpersonal matters
- Anything said or done during sessions

### The Only Exception

I understand that confidentiality may be broken **only** in the following circumstances:

1. **Imminent harm to self:** A member discloses active suicidal intent with a specific plan
2. **Imminent harm to others:** A member discloses intent to harm another person
3. **Abuse of vulnerable persons:** A member discloses ongoing abuse of a child or vulnerable adult

In these cases, I am ethically obligated to act to prevent harm, which may include breaking confidentiality to contact emergency services, mental health professionals, or protective services.

### What I Will NOT Do

I agree that I will NOT:
- [ ] Discuss other members' shares with my partner, family, or friends
- [ ] Discuss other members' shares with my therapist (general themes only, no identifying details)
- [ ] Share identifying details even if I anonymize the story
- [ ] Post about the group or its members on social media
- [ ] Bring up another member's share outside the Cell without their explicit permission

### The Spirit of Confidentiality

I understand that the purpose of confidentiality is to create a container where vulnerability is possible. Even if I'm not "caught" violating confidentiality, the violation still damages the container and my own integrity.

## SECTION 2: NO ADVICE-GIVING

### The Agreement

I agree not to give unsolicited advice, suggestions, solutions, or "fixes" to other members. When someone shares a difficulty, I will:

- Listen fully
- Witness their experience
- Respond with "Thank you for sharing" or similar acknowledgment
- Resist the urge to offer perspective, tools, or recommendations

### Why This Matters

I understand that unsolicited advice:
- Often reflects the giver's anxiety more than the receiver's needs
- Creates obligation and pressure on the receiver
- Prevents the speaker from fully processing their own experience
- Destroys the equality of the container

### What I CAN Do

If I genuinely believe I have something that might help:
- [ ] I will wait until after the session
- [ ] I will ask: "Would you be open to a suggestion?"
- [ ] If they say no, I will accept that completely
- [ ] I will not take their "no" personally

### What I CANNOT Do

- [ ] Offer advice during the session
- [ ] Frame advice as questions ("Have you tried...?")
- [ ] Share "what worked for me" unless explicitly requested
- [ ] Offer interpretations of others' experiences

## SECTION 3: SCOPE OF PRACTICE

### What This Cell IS

I understand that this Frequency Warrior Cell is:
- A peer support container for truth-telling and shared practice
- A space for witnessing and being witnessed
- An experimental structure for mutual accountability
- A complement to (not replacement for) professional support

### What This Cell IS NOT

I understand that this Cell is NOT:
- [ ] Therapy or counseling
- [ ] Medical or psychiatric treatment
- [ ] Crisis intervention or suicide prevention
- [ ] Spiritual direction or religious guidance
- [ ] Professional coaching
- [ ] Financial, legal, or career advice
- [ ] A substitute for intimate friendships or romantic partnership

### My Responsibility

I agree to:
- [ ] Seek professional help for clinical mental health needs
- [ ] Not rely on the Cell for crisis support
- [ ] Maintain relationships outside the Cell for comprehensive support
- [ ] Be honest about whether my needs exceed what the Cell can provide

### If I'm in Crisis

If I am experiencing suicidal thoughts, psychosis, or other acute mental health crisis:
- [ ] I will contact a mental health professional
- [ ] I will call a crisis line (988 in the US)
- [ ] I will go to an emergency room if needed
- [ ] I will inform the Cell of my situation without expecting them to resolve it

## SECTION 4: TRAUMA AWARENESS

### Understanding Trauma

I understand that:
- Some members may have trauma histories
- Trauma responses (dissociation, panic, shutdown) can occur unexpectedly
- The Cell is not designed to process trauma directly

### If I Become Activated

If I experience dissociation, panic, or overwhelm during a session, I agree to:
- [ ] Signal the facilitator (hand raise, verbal "I need to pause")
- [ ] Use grounding techniques (feel feet on floor, name what I see, breathe)
- [ ] Step out of the room if needed
- [ ] Not push through distress to "finish my share"

### Grounding Support

I understand that other members may offer simple grounding support:
- Reminding me to feel my feet
- Suggesting we breathe together
- Offering water or a cool cloth

I understand this is not treatment—just human support.

### If Trauma Keeps Surfacing

If I find that I am frequently activated in sessions, I agree to:
- [ ] Recognize that I may need trauma-specific therapy
- [ ] Consider whether the Cell is the right container for me right now
- [ ] Discuss this with the group rather than hiding it
- [ ] Prioritize my safety over my attendance

## SECTION 5: BOUNDARIES AROUND INTIMACY

### Appropriate Intimacy

I understand that deep sharing can create feelings of closeness. I agree that:
- [ ] Intimacy within the Cell serves the practice, not personal attachment
- [ ] I will not use the Cell to meet unmet attachment needs
- [ ] I will maintain appropriate boundaries with other members outside sessions

### Outside Relationships

If I have a pre-existing relationship with another member (friend, colleague, partner):
- [ ] I will be mindful of how that affects group dynamics
- [ ] I will not share things in the Cell that would violate the other person's privacy
- [ ] I will address any conflicts through the Cell process, not outside of it

### Romantic/Sexual Boundaries

I understand that:
- [ ] Romantic or sexual relationships between members can complicate the container
- [ ] If such a relationship develops, it should be disclosed to the group
- [ ] The group may need to discuss how this affects safety and dynamics
- [ ] In some cases, one or both members may need to step back from the Cell

## SECTION 6: PHYSICAL SAFETY

### Meeting Location

I agree to:
- [ ] Ensure meeting locations are physically safe and accessible
- [ ] Inform someone outside the Cell of my whereabouts
- [ ] Leave immediately if I ever feel physically unsafe

### Health Considerations

I agree to:
- [ ] Inform the group of any medical conditions that might require response (allergies, seizures, etc.)
- [ ] Not engage in practices that are medically contraindicated for me
- [ ] Take responsibility for my own physical wellbeing

## SECTION 7: EXIT PROTOCOLS

### Leaving the Cell

I understand that I may leave the Cell at any time. If I choose to leave, I agree to:
- [ ] Inform the group (not just disappear)
- [ ] Share what I'm comfortable sharing about my reasons
- [ ] Honor confidentiality even after departure
- [ ] Not recruit other members to leave with me

### If Asked to Leave

If the group asks me to leave because I cannot hold the agreements:
- [ ] I will receive this as information, not punishment
- [ ] I will honor the confidentiality agreement even if I feel hurt
- [ ] I will seek appropriate support elsewhere

### If the Cell Dissolves

If the Cell chooses to dissolve:
- [ ] I will participate in a closing session if possible
- [ ] I will honor what was shared and what we built together
- [ ] I will take time before joining or forming a new Cell
