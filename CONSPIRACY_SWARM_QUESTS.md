## QUEST 1: "THE MISSING NOOSE"
**Type:** Main Story | **Difficulty:** Introductory | **Estimated Time:** 45-60 minutes

### Overview
The official narrative says Epstein died by suicide. But the autopsy report has pages missing—pages that vanished the same night a certain correctional officer received an envelope with no return address. Your first lead is a grieving ME's assistant who knows too much.

| 3 | Locate the redacted autopsy pages | File Cabinet Delta | Surveillance system must be disabled |
| 4 | Extract without triggering alarms | Exfiltration route | Stealth recommended |
| 5 | Analyze the missing pages with your contact | Safehouse | Return to quest giver |

## QUEST 2: "TWO Gs"
**Type:** Main Story | **Difficulty:** Moderate | **Prerequisite:** Complete "The Missing Noose"

### Overview
Ghislaine Maxwell had two photos of herself taken on the same day—one in London, one in New York. The timestamps are 40 minutes apart. The impossible gap. Someone is lying about where she was, and the answer connects to who she was meeting. The trail leads to a photo lab that doesn't exist and a developer who never developed anything.

| 4 | Infiltrate the front company | Upper East Side brownstone | Stealth or social disguise |

```
Sienna Vance. That's who I found in the darkroom. Surrounded

The Maxwell in London—that was the real one. The LA photo

while her duplicate got drive-thru?

The answer's in the flight manifests. She wasn't flying

Epstein's island. Three days before he died.

She went to say goodbye. Or to make sure he couldn't 
say anything at all.
```

## QUEST 3: "FOLLOW THE TENT STAKES"

Something that was built, used, and then erased so thoroughly that even the terrain looks wrong. The trail leads to a black site that officially burned down in 2004. Unofficially, it's still receiving electricity.

```
JOURNAL ENTRY #3

The desert doesn't forget. Even when they bulldoze the 
barracks and haul away the barbed wire, the land remembers. 
The satellite images from 2003 show a compound. 2023 shows 
undisturbed desert. But the ground-penetrating radar I 
borrowed from a friend at USGS tells a different story.

There's something underground. Still there. Still occupied.

35.2014° N, 111.6542° W. Camp Sunrise. Listed as destroyed 
in a fire, 2004. The official report says 14 casualties. 
Training accident. But the electrical records show a steady 
2.4 megawatt draw every month since 2004. Someone's paying 
the power bill for a burned-down building.

I found the entrance under a rusted water tank. Staircase 
going down, down, down into the earth. The air smelled 
like ozone and bleach.

The Warden—Colonel Elias Vorhees—found me in the server 
room. Didn't even reach for his weapon at first. Just 
looked sad. Tired. "You're not the first to find this 
place," he said. "You won't be the first to disappear 
in it."

He believed in what he was doing. That was the worst part. 
Not a monster—just a man who'd been told the monsters 
were outside, and his job was to keep them there. Even 
if he had to become one himself.

The files I pulled... they're comprehensive. Every person 
who ever entered Camp Sunrise. Every "enhanced interrogation." 
Every experiment. Some names I recognized. A Supreme Court 
justice, visiting in 2006. A current senator, photographed 
with a prisoner in 2011. A billionaire, there last month.

They weren't just hiding crimes. They were monetizing them.

But the survivor in Cell 7... that's what I'll see when 
I close my eyes. Twenty years in the dark. They'd 
forgotten he existed. The system had swallowed him 
whole, and no one was looking anymore.

I carried him out. He weighed nothing. Kept whispering 
about "the children" and "the tents." About stakes in 
the ground that marked where they buried the ones who 
didn't survive the experiments.

Tent stakes in the desert. Markers for a mass grave that 
doesn't exist on any map.

I need to find those stakes.
```
