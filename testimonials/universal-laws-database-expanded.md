- Tesla: "Think in terms of energy, frequency and vibration"

**Modern Physics**
- Einstein: E=mc² (matter is energy)
- Quantum mechanics: Probability waves
- String theory: Everything as vibrating strings

1. **Everything Vibrates**

   - Beta (13-30 Hz): Active thinking

### Practical Applications

**Raising Vibration:**

- Nature: Earth frequencies (Schumann 7.83 Hz)
- Meditation: Stillness allows natural rise
- Connection: Love as highest frequency
- Creativity: Expression raises vibration
- Gratitude: Opens heart frequency

**SynSync Technology:**
- Direct application of Law of Vibration

### Alternate Names
- Law of Karma
- Law of Sowing and Reaping

### Core Principle

### Origins and Sources

**Ancient Indian**
- Rigveda: Early karma concepts (1500 BCE)
- Upanishads: "As you sow, so shall you reap"
- Bhagavad Gita: "To action alone have you a right"
- Buddhist Pali Canon: Karma as intention

**Hermetic**
- Kybalion: "Every Cause has its Effect"
- Emerald Tablet: Cyclical causation
- Divine order through law

**Abrahamic**
- Bible: "Whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap"
- Quran: "Whoever does an atom's weight of good will see it"
- Jewish: Measure for measure

### Key Teachings

1. **Inevitability**
   - Effects MUST follow causes
   - No action without consequence
   - Mathematical precision

2. **Correspondence**
   - Like produces like
   - Quality of cause determines quality of effect
   - Internal and external connected

3. **Types of Karma**
   - Sanchita: Accumulated from past lives
   - Prarabdha: Ripening in current life (fate)
   - Kriyamana: Being created now (free will)

4. **Intention Matters**
   - Karma = intention, not just action
   - "Intention, monks, is karma"
   - Why you do something matters as much as what

### Confidence Tag
- **Behavior → consequence:** Established
- **Multilife karma:** Speculative

- No absolute frame of reference
- Observer affects observation

- Protagoras: "Man is the measure of all things"
- Nietzsche: Beyond good and evil
- Existentialism: We create meaning

**Spiritual**
- Stoicism: It's not events that disturb us, but our judgments
- Buddhist: Second arrow (suffering about suffering)
- Taoist: Good and bad are provisional

### Key Teachings

1. **No Absolute Good/Bad**
   - Context determines value
   - Today's tragedy is tomorrow's gift
  - "This too shall pass"

2. **Comparison Creates Suffering**

   - Choice of comparison target matters

3. **Frame Determines Experience**
  - Same event, different stories
  - Cognitive reframing
   - "Is this happening TO me or FOR me?"

4. **The Observer Position**
   - Step back from identification
  - See the bigger picture
   - Multiple perspectives available

### Confidence Tag
- **Relativity in physics:** Established
- **Meaning as constructed:** Established (psychology)
- **No absolute good/bad:** Experimental/philosophical

4. **The Inversion Principle**
