## 3. DATA FUSION CAPABILITIES

### Palantir Gotham (Government/Military Platform)
Gotham is Palantir's flagship intelligence platform, designed as "the ultimate tool of surveillance"

**Core Capabilities:**
- **Data Integration**: Combines data from virtually any source - drones, satellites, intelligence reports, social media, arrest records, license plate readers, CCTV footage, medical records, financial data, call detail records (CDRs)
- **Social Network Analysis (SNA)**: Maps relationships between people, places, cars, weapons, addresses, social media posts
- **Geospatial Intelligence**: Real-time geographic tracking, movement analysis, hotspot identification
- **Link Analysis**: Visual representation of connections between data points
- **Pattern-of-Life Analysis**: Tracks behavioral patterns over time
- **Real-Time Processing**: Processes heterogeneous data streams in real-time
- **Common Operating Picture (COP)**: Unified dashboard for commanders and analysts

**Technical Architecture:**
- Sits on top of existing systems without requiring underlying data infrastructure changes
- Federated access management for multi-level security environments
- REST API and OAuth2 authentication
- Processes structured and unstructured data including audio and video

### Palantir Foundry (Commercial/Enterprise Platform)
- **Purpose**: Data operating system for enterprises and government agencies
- **Function**: Integrates operational data from production, supply chains, logistics
- **Government Use**: Now embedded across federal agencies for data fusion and migration

### Apollo and AIP
- **Apollo**: Deployment and data integration platform for continuous software updates
- **AIP (Artificial Intelligence Platform)**: Brings generative AI and automation into surveillance workflows

## 4. PREDICTIVE POLICING AND PRE-CRIME ALGORITHMS

### The New Orleans "Pre-Crime" Program (2012-2018)
- **Nature**: Secret predictive policing program without public knowledge or city council oversight
- **Data Sources**: 911 calls, arrest records, traffic stops, social media monitoring, field interview cards (70,000+ collected in 2011-2012), jailhouse phone calls, ballistics databases
- **Methodology**: Social network analysis to predict likelihood individuals would commit violence or become victims
- **Output**: List of ~3,900 people at highest risk of gun violence involvement
- **Key Figure**: Jeff Asher, former CIA intelligence agent who ran the program
- **Marketing Use**: Palantir used this "philanthropic" partnership to solicit contracts with other cities

### LAPD Operation LASER
- **System**: Created "Chronic Offender Bulletins" with "chronic offender scores"
- **Process**: 
  1. Initial screening by "crime intelligence analyst" 
  2. "Workup" phase using Palantir software to gather criminal history, affiliations, license plate reader data, social media
  3. Algorithm calculates risk score
  4. Officers send letters and conduct door-knocks to inform targets of monitoring
- **Impact**: Disproportionately targeted minority neighborhoods; created "racist feedback loop"
- **Outcome**: Program scrapped after public outcry

### The "NOLA Model"
- Based on Yale professor Andrew Papachristos's research tracking violence as a "communicable disease" through networks
- Claimed to identify 30-40% of shooting victims before they were shot
- Used for CeaseFire program - carrot-and-stick approach combining threats of prosecution with social services

### Palantir's 2014 Patent
- "Computer-based crime risk forecasting system"
- Claims to generate crime risk forecasts allowing "proactive approach to law enforcement"
- Enables predicting crime locations AND individuals likely to commit crimes

### International Predictive Policing
- **Denmark**: System uses license plate readers, CCTV, police reports to predict terrorism likelihood
- **Germany**: Hessendata platform ruled unconstitutional by German Federal Constitutional Court (2023) for violating "informational self-determination"
- **UK**: Nectar project integrating criminal records, mental health reports, children's data, victim statements, financial activity, political views

### Algorithmic Bias Issues
- Internal NOPD analysis (2016): Palantir algorithms flagged Black neighborhoods at rates 4.5x higher than white neighborhoods, even controlling for crime rates
- Research finding: "Predictive policing... is aptly named: it is predicting future policing, not future crime"
- Bias compounds: Biased training data leads to biased predictions, which justify biased policing decisions, creating feedback loops
